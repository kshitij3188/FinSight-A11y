# How do I declare my bunq account to the local tax authorities?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/how-do-i-declare-my-bunq-account-to-the-local-tax-authorities
**Topics:** Signup, France 🇫🇷

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈**

If you file a tax return in a country outside the Netherlands, you may need to submit information about your balances and/or earned Interest. Our aim is to ensure that everyone has the necessary information to complete their tax reports promptly and accurately.

### Why do I need to report my bunq accounts in my tax return?

bunq is a bank registered in the Netherlands. This means that if you live in France, Ireland, Spain or Germany, you need to report the max balances and interest you’ve earned as foreign Bank Accounts. You need to do this regardless of whether your IBANs start with NL, FR, DE, ES or IE. You can find all the information on balances, account numbers and Interest on your Annual Statement [here](<./how-do-i-export-my-annual-account-overview>).

## Who does this apply to?

#### Declaring Bank Accounts

Users with verified Personal or Business accounts residing in Germany, France, Ireland, and Spain should declare their bunq Bank Accounts to the tax authorities.

#### Declaring gained interest

Additionally, users residing in Germany, France, Ireland, and Spain who perceive MassInterest should declare the interest gained throughout the year to the local tax authorities.

## Why should I report my bunq Bank Account and interest gained?

If you're living in one of the above-mentioned countries, you’ll have to fill in your bunq accounts within your annual income/corporate tax return. Regardless of the IBAN type, failure to declare these balances and interest to your local authority can lead to serious consequences 

The Bank Accounts of Dutch users are the only ones that will be automatically declared in their online tax reporting. That’s why we want to make sure all other users from Germany, France, Ireland, and Spain report their Bank Accounts or interest gained throughout the year on time.

**Note:** We don't support the Freistellungsauftrag exemption and German users should declare their gained interest rate accordingly.

## Which form do I need to fill out based on the country I live in?

  * **🇫🇷 France** \- [Formulaire N° 3916 - 3916](<http://impots.gouv.fr/>)

  * **🇮🇪 Ireland** \- Form 11 through [Revenue Online Service (ROS)](<https://www.ros.ie/ros-registration-web/ros-registration;rjsessionid=DD5B80DE6F25AD0375CE0FC191084DAB?execution=e1s1>)

  * **🇪🇸 Spain** \- [Modelo 720](<https://sede.agenciatributaria.gob.es/Sede/en_gb/procedimientoini/GI34.shtml>)

  * **🇩🇪Germany** \- [Form management system (FMS)](<https://www.formulare-bfinv.de/>) or online through [Elektronische Steuererklärung](<https://www.elster.de/eportal/start>). German users need to declare the interest they’ve gained throughout the year only.

## What information do I have to fill in?

When reporting your Bank Account to your local tax authorities, make sure to provide the following information:

#### France

  * Bank Account details (date of opening, type of account, IBAN)

  * bunq’s name and address

  * Maximum balance during the reporting period

  * Interest earned

#### Ireland

  * Bank Account details (date of opening, type of account, IBAN, currency)

  * bunq’s name and address

  * the amount of money you lodged to open the account

  * any person or company in Ireland that helped you in opening the account

  * Interest earned

#### Spain

  * Bank Account details (date of opening, type of account, IBAN)

  * bunq’s name and address

  * Maximum balance for as of December 31st of the relevant tax year

  * Interest earned through the year

#### Germany

  * Financial income received within the tax year (annual statement, including interest information).

## What’s the submission deadline?

Each Tax Authority has its own month as a deadline. Thus, we strongly recommend checking this matter on your local Tax Authority’s website.
