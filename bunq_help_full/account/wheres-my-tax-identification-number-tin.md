# Where's my Tax Identification Number (TIN)?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/wheres-my-tax-identification-number-tin

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

A **Tax Identification Number (TIN)** is a personal number identifier issued by fiscal authorities in countries where you're a tax resident. Most banks require your TIN immediately, but we give you up to 90 days to submit it — making account set-up easy and stress-free!

**Keep in mind** : you'll need to provide your TIN for each country where you’re a tax resident, including the country you currently reside in.

**Helpful tips** 💡 

  * If you’re a tax resident in more than one country, please provide a TIN for each country - this can include countries outside the EEA

  * Your TIN should contain only alphanumeric characters (no spaces or special characters like - / . )

  * A personal TIN is for individual tax purposes and is not the same as a business VAT number — for personal account please provide your personal TIN

## What happens if you don’t submit your TIN in time?

If you miss the 90-day deadline, your account will be temporarily restricted until you provide your TIN and a valid required document showing your **Tax Identification Number**.

You can upload this directly in the App or contact our Support Team via the secure, in-app Support chat.

## How can I provide my Tax Identification Number (TIN)?

  1. Tap on your Profile in the top left corner

  2. Tap on the TIN event under "Activity"

  3. Provide us with the relevant proof of your TIN

If you wish to provide a new or additional Tax Identification Number (TIN), please contact our Support Center and make sure to attach the required proof of your updated TIN.

## Your TIN: what it is and where to locate it

Name, format and how Tax Identification Number (TIN) is issued can vary by country. In some countries, a TIN is automatically issued at birth, when you register as a resident or when you register with the tax authority. Where you can find your TIN also depends on the country that issued it — it’s often shown on identity documents, official tax documents or government letters. 

Wondering what is your TIN and where to find it? Check out the tips below 👇

#### Austria 🇦🇹

  * **TIN name:** Steuernummer (sometimes referred to as “Steuerliche Identifikationsnummer”)

  * **Format:** 9 digits

Your Steuernummer can be found on official tax documents such as income tax assessments or letters from the local tax office. If your gross annual income does not exceed €11,000 and you’re not required to file a tax return, you may not have a Steuernummer and can request an exemption by providing a supporting document.

**Important** : The Steuernummer may be reissued if you move to a different district/tax office because it’s tied to the “issuing office”.

#### Belgium 🇧🇪

  * **TIN name:** National Register Number (Numéro national / Rijksregisternummer)

  * **Format:** 11 digits

Your National Register Number can be found on your identity documents, official population register documents, or correspondence from the Belgian tax authorities.

#### Bulgaria 🇧🇬

  * **TIN name:** ЕГН (EGN) for citizens; ЛНЧ (LNCh) for non-residents

  * **Format:** 10 digits

Your Bulgarian TIN can be found on your identity documents, residence permit, or official documents issued by Bulgarian authorities, such as tax or registration documents.

#### Croatia 🇭🇷

  * **TIN name:** OIB (Osobni identifikacijski broj)

  * **Format:** 11 digits

Your OIB can be found on your identity documents, tax returns, or correspondence from the Croatian Tax Administration.

#### Cyprus 🇨🇾

  * **TIN name:** Tax Identification Code (Αριθμός Φορολογικής Ταυτότητας – ΑΦΤ)

  * **Format:** 8 digits and 1 letter

Your Cypriot TIN can be found on tax assessments, employer forms, or official correspondence from the Cyprus tax authorities.

#### Czech Republic 🇨🇿

  * **TIN name:** Rodné číslo  
**Format:** 10 digits

Your Czech TIN can be found on your identity documents, residence permit card or official tax correspondence. 

#### Denmark 🇩🇰

  * **TIN name:** CPR number (CPR-nummer)

  * **Format:** 10 digits

Your CPR number can be found on your identity documents, health insurance card, residence registration documents, or correspondence from Danish authorities.

#### Estonia 🇪🇪

  * **TIN name:** Isikukood**  
Format:** 11 digits

Your Isikukood can be found on your identity documents, residence permit, or official documents issued by Estonian authorities.

#### Finland 🇫🇮

  * **TIN name:** Finnish Personal Identity Code (Henkilötunnus)

  * **Format:** 11 characters

Your Finnish TIN can be found on your identity documents, Kela card, or official correspondence from Finnish authorities.

#### France 🇫🇷

  * **TIN name:** Numéro fiscal de référence

  * **Format:** 13 digits

Your French TIN can be found on your tax assessment, tax return, or official letters from the French tax authority.

#### Germany 🇩🇪

  * **TIN name:** Persönliche**** Steueridentifikationsnummer (IdNr)

  * **Format:** 11 digits

You can find your German TIN in several places: on the document you receive from the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern after registering your address for the first time, on your pay slip (Gehaltsabrechnung), on your salary tax statement (Lohnsteuerbescheinigung), on your income tax assessment (Steuerbescheid), or on your Entgeltabrechnung. 

Your employer or tax advisor can also provide it. If needed, you can request your TIN from the German tax authorities via their official website — obtaining your TIN may take around 4 weeks.

**Important:** For your personal bank account, only the Persönliche Steueridentifikationsnummer (IdNr) is accepted. The Steuernummer or Umsatzsteuernummer cannot be used!

#### Greece 🇬🇷

  * **TIN name:** AFM (Αριθμός Φορολογικού Μητρώου)

  * **Format:** 9 digits

Your Greek AFM can be found on Tax Completeness Status, Tax Refund Status, tax returns, official tax correspondence, or documents issued by the Greek tax authorities.

#### Hungary 🇭🇺

  * **TIN name:** Tax Identification Code (Adóazonosító jel)

  * **Format:** 10 digits

Your Hungarian Tax Identification Code can be found on your tax card (Adóigazolvány), official tax documents, or correspondence from the Hungarian tax authority (NAV). It is usually issued automatically when you register with the authorities or start working in Hungary. 

#### Ireland 🇮🇪

  * **TIN name:** PPS Number

  * **Format:** 7 digits and 1–2 letters

Your PPS Number can be found on correspondence from the Irish tax or social protection authorities.

#### Italy 🇮🇹

  * **TIN name:** Codice Fiscale

  * **Format:** 16 characters

Your Codice Fiscale can be found on your health card, tax card, ID documents, or official correspondence from Italian authorities.

#### Latvia 🇱🇻

  * **TIN name:** Personas Codas

  * **Format:** 11 digits

Your Latvian TIN can be found on your identity documents or official correspondence from Latvian authorities.

#### Lithuania 🇱🇹

  * **TIN name:** Asmens kodas

  * **Format:** 11 digits

Your Asmens kodas can be found on your identity documents or official correspondence from Lithuanian authorities.

#### Luxembourg 🇱🇺

  * **TIN name:** Numéro d’identification national

  * **Format:** 13 digits

Your Luxembourg TIN can be found on tax correspondence or official documents issued by authorities.

#### Malta 🇲🇹

  * **TIN name:** Identity Card Number

  * **Format:** Citizen: 7 digits + 1 letter; Resident: 9 digits

Your Maltese TIN can be found on your identity documents, tax assessments or correspondence from the Maltese tax authorities.

#### The Netherlands 🇳🇱

  * **TIN name:** BSN (Burgerservicenummer)

  * **Format:** 9 digits

Your BSN can be found on your identity documents, residence permit, or official government and tax authorities correspondence.

#### Poland 🇵🇱

  * **TIN name:** PESEL

  * **Format:** 11 digits

Your PESEL can be found on your identity documents or official correspondence from Polish authorities.

#### Portugal 🇵🇹

  * **TIN name:** NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal)  
**Format:** 9 digits

Your NIF can be found on your identity documents, tax documents or correspondence from Portuguese tax authorities.

#### Romania 🇷🇴

  * **TIN name:** CNP

  * **Format:** 13 digits

Your CNP can be found on your identity documents or tax-related correspondence.

#### Slovakia 🇸🇰

  * **TIN name:** Citizens: Rodné číslo; Residents: Identifikačné číslo

  * **Format:** 10 digits (at times 9 digits)

Your Slovak TIN can be found on your identity documents, residence permit card or official tax correspondence.

#### Slovenia 🇸🇮

  * **TIN name:** Davčna številka  
**Format:** 8 digits

Your Slovenian TIN can be found on your identity documents, certificate of residence, tax documents or correspondence from tax authorities.

#### Spain 🇪🇸

  * **TIN name:** Citizens: Documento Nacional de Identidad (DNI); Residents: Número de Identificación de Extranjero (NIE)

  * **Format:** 9 characters

Your Spanish TIN can be found on your identity documents or official correspondence from Spanish authorities.

#### Sweden 🇸🇪

  * **TIN name:** Personnummer

  * **Format:** 10 digits

Your Personnummer can be found on your identity documents or correspondence from Swedish authorities.

#### United States 🇺🇸

  * **TIN name:** Social Security Number (SSN)

  * **Format:** 9 digits

You can find your U.S. TIN on several official documents: on your Social Security card, on your ITIN assignment letter from the IRS, on pay slips, W-2 forms, 1099 forms, or U.S. tax returns. For more information please check out our [SSN help page](<./should-i-also-provide-my-social-security-number-\(ssn\)>). 

## How do I update my Tax Identification Number (TIN)?

To provide us with your new or additional Tax Identification Number (TIN), please reach out via our Support Center. Make sure to attach the necessary proof of your updated TIN.

## Do you have a Business account with us?

Please provide us your personal TIN rather than your company number. This is because we always need to verify your Personal account in order to activate your Business account. Read more [here](<https://help.bunq.com/articles/why-do-you-need-a-personal-account-with-your-business-account>).

## What if I don’t provide my TIN in time? 

If you weren’t able to add your TIN in the first 90 days, your account access will be **temporarily restricted.** This means that when you open your bunq App, you’ll see a dedicated screen to help you fill in your TIN. Think of it as a quick pit-stop before you can get back on the road. 

No worries though, getting full app access back is quick and easy. Here’s how: 

  1. **Submit your TIN** and you can get right back to easy banking with your bunq App straight away. 

  2. **Upload a document** supporting your tax exemption status. 

If you upload a supporting document, here’s what you can expect to happen next: 

  * **Review** : We’ll review your submission by the end of the same day. While you won’t be able to access your account _just yet_ , the app will open straight to the Support chat so you have a direct line to our Support Team. 

  * **Approved** : You’re all set – we accepted your documents! You can get right back to easy banking with your bunq App. 

  * **Action needed** : If your document submission doesn’t quite meet our acceptance policy, you’ll be able to re-submit your eligible documents through the same in-app flow.
