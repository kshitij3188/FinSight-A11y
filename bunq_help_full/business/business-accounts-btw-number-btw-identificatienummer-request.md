# Business accounts: BTW number (btw-identificatienummer) request

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/business-accounts-btw-number-btw-identificatienummer-request
**Topics:** Business, Signup, Netherlands 🇳🇱

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

As a bank, we may request your Dutch business's **BTW number (btw-identificatienummer)**. Here's a guide to help you locate it. In the Netherlands, businesses use two primary tax identification numbers:

  * **BTW-identificatienummer (BTW-ID):** This is your **VAT identification number** used for dealings with customers and suppliers. It typically follows the format: NL123456789B01.

  * **Omzetbelastingnummer:** This number is used for communication with the Dutch Tax Administration, especially concerning VAT returns.

## How can I registed my BTW-ID?

If you've misplaced your BTW-ID, follow these steps:

  1. **Access the portal:** Log in to Mijn Belastingdienst Zakelijk using your DigiD credentials.

  2. **Navigate to 'Btw':** Once logged in, click on the 'Btw' section.

  3. **Find your BTW-ID:** At the bottom, select 'Btw-identificatienummer'. Your **BTW-ID** and **Omzetbelastingnummer** will be displayed.

## Why does the bunq need my BTW number?

We ask for your **BTW (VAT) number** to verify your business identity and comply with Dutch and EU regulatory requirements, such as tax reporting and anti-fraud measures. It also helps us confirm your business registration with the Dutch Tax and Customs Administration (Belastingdienst).

## Is the BTW number the same as the KVK number?

No, they are different. The **KVK number** is your registration number with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce (Kamer van Koophandel). The **BTW number** is issued by the Dutch Tax Office and is used for VAT purposes.

## Where can I find my BTW number?

You can find your **BTW-ID** by logging into Mijn Belastingdienst Zakelijk with your DigiD. Under the “BTW” section, your number is listed under “Btw-identificatienummer.”

## What does a Dutch BTW number look like?

A typical **BTW-ID** follows this format:

  * NL123456789B01 “NL” is the country code, followed by 9 digits, and ending with a “B” and a 2-digit sequence.

## Can the bunq look up my BTW number for me?

Unfortunately, no. **BTW numbers are not publicly searchable.** For privacy and security reasons, you must retrieve and provide it yourself via the Belastingdienst portal.

## I’m a sole proprietor (eenmanszaak). Do I have a BTW number?

Yes, even sole proprietors receive a **BTW-ID** from the Belastingdienst. For privacy reasons, it no longer contains your BSN and is separate from your personal data.

## What if I don’t have a BTW number yet?

If you just registered your business, you should receive your **BTW-ID** within a few days via post from the Belastingdienst. If it’s delayed, contact them directly for support.
