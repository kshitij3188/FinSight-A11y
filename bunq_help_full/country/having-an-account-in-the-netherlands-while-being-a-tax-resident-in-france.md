# Having an account in the Netherlands while being a tax resident in France

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/having-an-account-in-the-netherlands-while-being-a-tax-resident-in-france
**Topics:** France 🇫🇷

## General Information on Opening an Account at a Bank in the European Union

First of all, know that it is completely legal to open a bank account outside of France, notably in the Netherlands - IBAN starting with NL. The only thing to do is to declare your account along with your income. Of course, we will send you an email when the time comes to specify all the information to be disclosed based on your situation.

## Discrimination Based on IBAN

Since the establishment of the SEPA zone (**Single Euro Payments Area**), the member countries of this agreement have agreed on a system based on IBAN to simplify international transactions. By refusing your IBAN, merchants or employers are in violation of the European law of February 1, 2016 ([Art. 9, European Directive No. 260/2012](<https://eur-lex.europa.eu/LexUriServ/LexUriServ.do?uri=OJ:L:2012:094:0022:0037:fr:PDF>)). If you find yourself in a situation where your IBAN is refused, we encourage you to contact [the national authority](<https://finance.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2022-10/sepa-compliance-competent-authorities_en.pdf>) in force by sending them as much information as possible regarding the situation, the merchant, or the employer involved.

## Declare Your Account Along with Your Income

If you are resident in France or Monaco, you must declare accounts opened, held, used, or closed abroad. 

You must declare accounts opened outside of France to a banking institution or any other establishment or person (notary, stockbroker, etc.).

You are required to declare, whether you are the account holder or a beneficiary of a power of attorney.

The dated and signed declaration must be attached to your income tax return.

[Click here](<https://www.impots.gouv.fr/formulaire/3916/declaration-par-un-resident-dun-compte-letranger-ou-dun-contrat-de-capitalisation-o>) to download the Cerfa 3916 form for declaring your accounts abroad. You will find more information on how to declare your bunq account to the French tax authorities at this link.

## Using bunq Business

Similarly, you must declare your business account by filling out the **Cerfa 3916 form** when you complete your income tax return on your Cerfa 2042 form. The only difference is that you must declare this account as being related to a professional activity.
