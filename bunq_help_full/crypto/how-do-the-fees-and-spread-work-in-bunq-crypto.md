# How do the Fees and Spread work in bunq Crypto?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/how-do-the-fees-and-spread-work-in-bunq-crypto
**Topics:** Crypto, Pricing

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

We're here to help you navigate our crypto feature with confidence! 

Let's clearly break down the fees you'll encounter and explain why the price you see when buying may differ slightly from the price you see displayed on the **Crypto** screen.

## What fees do I pay? 🤔

Every time you buy, sell, or swap crypto, you pay a small service fee. This depends on your bunq plan:

bunq Free: 1.99% 

bunq Core: 0.99%

bunq Pro: 0.49%

bunq Elite: 0.25%

We always show you the total price upfront in the app before you confirm your payment. 

This way, you always know what you’re paying. No surprises. 🙌

## Why does the price differ when I check the cryptocurrency details and when I buy?

When buying cryptocurrency like Bitcoin, what you pay includes a **spread**. A spread is the difference between the buy price (what you pay when buying) and the sell price (what you’d get if you sold right away). This is standard across all retail crypto platforms.

This exists because of how markets work — just like with currency exchange or stock trading, there’s always a small gap between the highest price that buyers are willing to pay and the lowest price that sellers are asking for. The gap between these two prices is the spread.

If you notice that the value of the cryptocurrency is slightly less than what you paid, it's likely just the spread and normal price fluctuations. It does not necessarily reflect a market drop or loss in value.

## Frequently Asked Questions

  * **How do I know what I’m paying?**

Before confirming any crypto payments in the bunq app, you’ll see the final price with all service fees included. You're always fully informed of what you’re agreeing to.

  
  

  * **Why can't I sell for the same price I bought?**

Because of the spread, if you sell immediately, you’ll likely get slightly less than what you just paid. This is standard across all trading platforms and reflects the market.
