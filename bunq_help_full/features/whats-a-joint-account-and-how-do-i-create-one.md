# What's a Joint Account and how do I create one?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/whats-a-joint-account-and-how-do-i-create-one

---

Hey bunqers 🌈,

Sharing a Bank Account with friends or loved ones has never been easier! Joint Accounts allow multiple users to manage shared finances effortlessly. This feature is available for **Personal bunq Core, Pro, and Elite users** 📲

### What's a Joint Account?

A Joint Account allows 2 or more legal owners to share and manage a payment account together.

✔ Instant notifications for all transactions.  
✔ All funds are shared, including any negative balances ([Learn more](<https://together.bunq.com/d/24781>)).

📝 **Requirements:** Each co-owner must have their [own bunq account](<https://together.bunq.com/d/1015>) before creating a Joint Account.

## How do I create a Joint Account?

You can invite both bunq and non-bunq users. If your partner is a non-bunq user, they will need to sign up and accept the invite. Here’s how you can create a Joint account:

#### Existing user

Follow the steps mentioned below:

  1. Go to the 🏠 Home tab

  2. Tap on **Add an Extra Bank Account**

  3. Tap on "Joint Account"

  4. Select Partner(s) 

  5. Personalize your account 

  6. Done! ✅

#### New user

  1. Open the app

  2. Tap on “Get Started"

  3. Enter and confirm your phone number 

  4. Tap on **Sign up in 5 minutes**

  5. Select **For me and my partner**

  6. Fill in your personal information

  7. Add your partner’s information and send them an invite

  8. Start the verification process by verifying your identity

## How many people can I invite?

A Joint Account can have up to 10 people (you + 9 others), and **all co-owners share equal rights to the account**. Once a Joint Account is created, co-owners are permanently assigned and can’t be removed or changed.

## What’s included in our Personal accounts?

When you open your Personal accounts, you’ll each get:

  * 1 payment Bank Account

  * 1 Savings Account

  * 1 Joint Account, if you invite a co-owner to join directly from your sign-up

  * 1 Joint savings account, if you invite a co-owner to join directly from your sign-up.

## How can I accept a Joint Account invitation?

  1. Go to the 🏠 Home tab

  2. Select the pending the Joint Account

  3. Tap on the ✅ under the invitation

  4. “Accept Invite”

Once everyone accepts their invitations, your Joint Account will become active.

## How can I close a Joint Account?

  1. Go to the 🏠 Home tab

  2. Tap on the Joint Account

  3. Select Settings

  4. Tap on Close account

## Good to know

  * A Joint Account counts toward the 25-account limit for bunq Pro and bunq Elite, and the 5 Bank Account limit for bunq Core;

  * Joint Accounts will come with a Dutch (NL) IBAN by default. For sharing an account with an IBAN from a different country, the best approach is to open an [International Bank Account ](<https://help.bunq.com/articles/foreign-ibans>)and then grant [Shared Access](<https://help.bunq.com/articles/what-is-shared-access-and-how-to-use-it>) to the other person.

  * Once a Joint Account is closed, it can’t be reopened;

  * If one co-owner closes the Joint Account, it must be closed for all owners since the right to close it is shared equally;

  * If a Joint Account is set as the [billing account](<https://together.bunq.com/d/2388-billing-account>) invoices for the connected Personal accounts will also be deducted from it;

  * You can request a Switch Service for a Joint Account as explained [here](<https://together.bunq.com/d/4706>);

  * A Joint Account can’t be set up with a Business user, but you can Share Access to a Personal account through [Shared Access](<https://together.bunq.com/d/1982>);

  * In the Joint Account transaction history, you can see who made each payment by checking the profile picture next to the transaction, making it easier to track expenses;

  * bunq Free users can join unlimited Joint Accounts but can’t create them. These **do not count** toward the 3-account limit of the bunq Free plan.
