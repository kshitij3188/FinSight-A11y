# Why was my transaction not allowed?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/why-was-my-transaction-not-allowed
**Topics:** Payments

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

To protect you from potential fraud, we may block or set limits on certain payments. If you see a **Transaction Not Allowed** message, it means that we consider the payment to be high-risk and therefore didn’t approve it.

Our decision can be based on multiple factors, including:

  * The amount you’re trying to send

  * How often you make payments of this type

  * The recipient of the payment

  * Known fraud patterns and risk indicators

These measures are in place to protect you from financial loss due to scams and fraud.

### What should I do if my payment’s not approved?

  1. **Understand the block** : We stop the payment if we consider it high-risk.

  2. **Explore alternatives** : If the payment is legitimate, you may want to try a different payment method or contact the recipient for other options.

  3. **Reach out for help** : If you have any questions or need assistance, contact our [Support](<https://www.bunq.com/en-nl/link/support>). We’re here to help 🌈

## Why do we stop some transactions? 

Fraudsters use many tactics to trick people into sending money. By limiting or blocking high-risk transactions, we help protect you from:

  * **Help Desk fraud** : Scammers pretend to be your bank or a trusted company and ask you to install software like AnyDesk.

  * **Investment scams** : Fraudsters promise guaranteed profits through fake investments that sound too good to be true.

  * **Social engineering scams** : Criminals pressure you into making payments by pretending to be a friend, family member or authority figure.

‼️Important: bunq will never call you or ask you to install software on your device. If you receive a suspicious request, contact us directly through our secure in-app Support chat. Learn more about [spoofing and how to recognize it here](<https://help.bunq.com/articles/anti-fraud-guide-spoofing-the-dynamic-ways-of-scamming-nowadays-explained>).

## What if I've been scammed? 

We know that being scammed can be scary, we are here to help support you! If you think you’re a victim of fraud:

  * **Report it** : Let us know immediately via our [fraud report form](<https://bunq.com/contact/report>)​​.

  * **Stay informed** : Read our guide on recognizing [phishing and scams](<https://help.bunq.com/articles/anti-fraud-guide-whats-phishing-and-how-to-recognize-it>) to learn how to spot suspicious activity and stay safe.

Your safety is our top priority. If you need further help, you can contact our [Support](<https://www.bunq.com/link/support>).
