# When will my payment arrive?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/when-will-my-payment-arrive
**Topics:** Payments

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

This topic outlines the estimated time of arrival for incoming and outgoing payments.

We do our best to process your payments as soon as possible. If the processing of an incoming or outgoing payment takes longer than expected, the bunq app shows you the estimated delivery date.

## How long does my payment take to arrive?

  * Transfers between bunq accounts are[ instant⚡️](<https://together.bunq.com/d/40305>)

  * Transfers between your bunq account and external banks in euros, more specifically in the[ SEPA Region](<https://www.ecb.europa.eu/paym/integration/retail/sepa/html/index.en.html>), typically reach the recipient within 1 to 2 business days.

  * Direct debit payments are usually instant but can take up to 1-2 business days.

  * Wise payments are usually 2-5 business days, but can take up to a week due to weekends or public holidays.

  * For[ SWIFT payments](<https://together.bunq.com/d/4957>), the processing time is usually 5 days but can extend up to a week, due to the procedures of the other bank involved.

_That said, we use strict security measures to verify the legitimacy of outgoing and incoming payments. If you see that a pending incoming payment or your outgoing payment's estimated time of arrival (ETA) is tomorrow, it's possible that payments are being reviewed by us before they are processed. As such, it may take longer for your payment to arrive than you initially expected_.

## Why wasn't my payment sent instantly? 

Whenever you send money to an IBAN, we always try to send it as an Instant Payment so it arrives right away. If the receiving bank doesn’t support or accept **Instant Payments** , your transfer is automatically sent as a regular SEPA transfer instead. Typically in this case, your payment should arrive within **1-2 business days**.

So if your payment wasn’t instant, it’s simply because of the receiving bank’s setup. Rest assured, your money is safely on its way 💸

## How can I see the estimated arrival time of my outgoing payment?

If you’ve initiated a payment but it hasn’t arrived to your recipient yet, it’s considered a pending outgoing payment.

To see the estimated arrival time of your outgoing payment, follow these steps:

  1. Go to 🏠 Home

  2. Tap on the Bank Account you’ve made the payment from

  3. Tap on the payment

  4. ‘Estimated Delivery’ shows you if the payment has arrived or when the payment is expected to arrive.

## How can I see the estimated arrival time of my incoming payment?

A pending incoming payment is a payment that has been initiated but hasn't yet been fully processed and credited to your account. Think of it as a package that is on its way to you but hasn't been delivered yet.

If bunq receives an incoming payment and we are reviewing it, the app will show the payment with an estimated delivery date in the payment details. To see the estimated arrival time, follow these steps:

  1. Go to 🏠 Home

  2. Tap on the Bank Account where you expect the payment to arrive

  3. Tap on the payment

  4. ‘Estimated Delivery’ shows you when the payment is expected to arrive.

  5. Once the your incoming payment arrives, you’ll receive a notification 🔔

## Why is my payment taking longer than expected?

To ensure the secure processing of your payment, bunq and the other bank may need to exchange information on the payment. This interbank communication is a standard payment process to help verify payment details and ensure that funds reach their intended destination safely. While this process usually happens seamlessly in the background, very occasionally it can add some time to the processing of incoming or outgoing payments.

Other possible reasons for a longer payment processing time may be one of the following:

  * The cooperating bank has an ongoing incident or service outage (contact them for further investigation on your transfer and further solution);

  * The payment got stuck in the cooperating bank's system and has to be reversed from their side (contact them for further payment investigation on their side);

  * The payment got stuck in our system and has to be reversed from our side (start an in-app payment investigations as explained[ here](<https://together.bunq.com/d/46954>));

  * The payment didn't arrive, bounced back or is delayed because of service outage on our side (rare case scenario, if something like this happens we'll let you know immediately);

  * You changed your account’s daily limit before making a payment.

  * You logged in on a new device before making a payment.

  * The payment has otherwise raised our suspicion and we’re taking the necessary steps to verify its legitimacy. This review can take up to 48 hours.

  * Your payment might be delayed. Further information on the matter can be found[ here](<https://together.bunq.com/d/59881>).

### Rejected or returned payments

If a payment you sent is rejected or returned by the beneficiary bank, it may take up to 4 working days for the money to return to your account. In some cases, the process can take longer if the beneficiary bank performs additional checks.

If the beneficiary bank requests more information about your outgoing payment:

  * You can export your payment statement and share it directly with them.

If the payment was successfully sent from our side but the beneficiary bank cannot locate it, we require a statement confirming that they didn’t recieve the payment using the provided reference to investigate further.

## My payment hasn't arrived yet, what can I do?

Keep in mind that different payments have different estimated arrival times. If your payment isn’t immediately processed - no worries! In a majority of cases, the payments are slightly delayed and make it to the account within the processing timeframe. You can check the average estimated times in the _How long does my payment take to arrive?_ section above.

We strongly recommend waiting at least 2 days before initiating an in-app Payment Investigation as the money is likely to arrive within this timeframe.

## Which payment types are eligible for a Payment Investigation?

If your bank transfer payment is missing or is delayed beyond the 2 day timeframe, you can start a Payment Investigation. The following payments are eligible for a Payment Investigation:

  * Incoming bank transfers (SEPA or SWIFT)

  * Outgoing bank transfers you sent from bunq (SEPA or SWIFT)

  * Wise payments that haven’t arrived after the expected timeframe

Not eligible for a Payment Investigation:

  * Card payments and merchant refunds. You can use the chargeback flow instead — see How chargebacks work for card payments[ here](<./how-can-i-request-chargebacks-card-payment-refunds>).

## How do I initiate an in-app Payment Investigation?

**For Incoming payments**

If you’re waiting for an incoming payment (that isn't delayed) or when it has been returned to the sender, you can start an in-app Payment Investigation. To do so, see the steps below:

  1. Tap on your Profile in the top left corner

  2. Tap on 🟢 Support

  3. Make sure to share all of the following details with our instant support chat:

     * A quick description of the issue,

     * Your Bank Account details (IBAN),

     * The sender's Bank Account details (IBAN),

     * The payment date and amount,

     * A payment confirmation document (e.g., a bank statement),

     * A transaction ID (EBA ID if your transfer was made within the EEA or the UETR code if you made a SWIFT payment).

To help us locate your payment easily and quickly, we need all the details mentioned above. We can escalate your case to the relevant team only once we have this information. They will then review your case and continue working on a solution.

Further information on Payment Investigations can be found[ here](<https://together.bunq.com/d/46954>).

**For outgoing payments**

You can find the estimated delivery of an outgoing payment in the bunq app as described above. For payments that have raised our suspicion and are therefore being reviewed, you have 24 hours to cancel it. To do so, find the payment in your bunq app and tap on ‘Cancel this Payment’. The payment will be canceled and your balance will be restored.

If your payment hasn’t arrived after 2 days, you can:

  1. Open your bunq app.

  2. Select the payment in question.

  3. Export the statement: we generate statements which contain your and recipient’s IBANS, payment date and amount and transaction IDs (EBA ID or UETR Code) to help the recipient’s bank easily trace the payment.

  4. Send the exported statement to the recipient’s bank for verification.

  5. The recipient’s bank will check the statement and confirm they got the payment.

  6. If the recipient bank can’t locate the payment, they should provide a confirmation document to you.

  7. Reach out to bunq Support with the confirmation document from the recipient’s bank and the exported statement. Our Payments team will take it from there 🚀

## How can I request a Chargeback or recall a payment?

If you'd like to request a chargeback for a card payment, you can do so straight from the payment in your app. Check out[ How can I request a Chargeback?](<./how-can-i-request-chargebacks-card-payment-refunds>) to learn more about our Chargeback process.

If you'd like to recall a sent bank transfer, reach out to our Payments team via Support chat.
