# Stocks FAQ

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/stocks-faq
**Topics:** Frequently Asked Questions

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

Our Stocks FAQ guide is designed to tackle the most common questions about the Stocks Tab, giving you a clearer picture and a confidence boost as you kickstart your investment journey.

## What financial products can I invest in?

You can invest in a wide range of carefully selected financial products. This includes **stocks and Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)**. 

## Which account will be used for my trades and fees?

Trades and fees will be deducted from the **Bank Account you use to buy or sell your stocks**. You can always change the preferred Bank Account by following these steps:

  1. Go to the 📈 Stocks tab

  2. Select the Stock or ETF you’d like to buy, for example

  3. Tap on **Buy**

  4. Select the amount you want to buy 

  5. Select which Bank Account you'd prefer to buy from, and change the linked Bank Account

## How can I share my Stocks trades with friends?

Your Stocks trades are always **private by default.** You can share your Stocks trades with your contacts in the bunq App by enabling trade sharing. Here’s how you can share your Stocks trading: 

  1. Go to your Stocks tab

  2. Tap on ⚙️ **Settings** in the top right corner 

  3. Toggle on for **See Friends' Trades**

  4. All done!

When you turn on trade sharing: 

  * You’ll share your Stocks and Crypto trades with your contacts

  * You’ll see trades from your friends who have also chosen to share their trade activity. 

You can change your mind whenever you want. To turn trade sharing off, simply:

  1. Go to the **Crypto** or **Stocks** tab.

  2. Tap on ⚙️ **Settings**.

  3. Toggle off for **See Friends' Trades** to switch sharing off. 

You can learn more about sharing trades with friends [here](<./share-trades-with-friends>). 

## How do I receive dividends?

If you own a stock or ETF that gives out dividends, these will be automatically deposited into the **Bank Account you've selected**. If you want to change it, follow these steps:

  1. Go to 📈 Stocks

  2. Tap on ⚙️Settings

  3. Tap on **Paid to** under Dividends

  4. Change with a preferred Bank Account

## Why can’t I buy or sell a specific stock or ETF?

During periods of **significant market volatility** or when certain announcements greatly impact the price of one or more stocks, the **Exchange Supervisory Authority** has the power to stop the ability to trade certain stocks.It can also be the case that a stock has ongoing corporate actions that means it needs to halt trading.

## What is the “Annual Cost” I see on an ETF?

The "Annual Cost" you see associated with an Exchange Traded Fund (ETF) is also referred to as the **Total Expense Ratio (TER)**. It’s a fee that the provider of an Exchange Traded Fund (ETF) charges for managing the ETF portfolio. You don't need to pay this fee and worry about setting funds aside for this fee. Instead, it's automatically deducted by the ETF provider. It's important to note that the **Annual Cost can impact the overall returns on your investment**.

## How does tax payment work?

You’ll receive a **tax statement** in May. This statement includes the **capital gains you earned** , which need to be declared in your tax return. The document you get and when it arrives depends on your country. Here’s a quick guide:

  * 🇳🇱 **The Netherlands:** You'll receive a document showing the value of your portfolio as of January 1st for the tax year. We'll start sending these in batches from **March 10, 2026** , and they'll all be available by **April 15, 2026**.

  * **🇩🇪 Germany** : For our German bunqers, you'll get a detailed tax report with your positions, transactions, and dividends. You can expect to receive it between **March 15 and March 31, 2026**.

  * 🇪🇺**All Other Countries:** If you live anywhere else, your complete transaction history will be available by **May 15, 2026**. Don't worry if that seems late — the tax deadlines in countries like Spain, France, Ireland, Italy, Belgium, and Portugal are all after this date.

Keep in mind that most of the French, Italian and Spanish stocks have a government-mandated tax applied. The Financial Transaction Tax (FTT) tax will be deducted every time you buy these stocks. You will see it in the app before you confirm a purchase. More details on the FTT tax can be found [here](<./stocks-what-is-financial-transaction-tax-ftt>). 

You can easily find and download your tax statement by following these steps: 

  1. Open the bunq app and go to the **Stocks** tab

  2. Go to **Settings**

  3. Tap on **Documents**

  4. You'll find your ad-hoc tax-related documents will be right there, ready to download 

> **Note:** Please be aware that both bunq and Ginmon don’t hold responsibility for the submission of your tax return. This is a task that you must undertake yourself, adhering to the deadlines set by your country of residence. 

## Stocks tax payment by country

#### Austria: 

  * **Capital Gains Tax** : 27.5% flat rate on dividends and capital gains.

#### Belgium:

  * **Stock Exchange Tax (TOB):** 0.35% tax on stock purchases and sales.

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** Generally not taxed for individual investors, unless trades are considered speculative or professional.

#### France:

  * [**Financial Transaction Tax (FTT):**](<https://together.bunq.com/d/60852>) A 0.3% tax on the purchase of certain French stocks.

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** A flat 30% tax, which includes both income tax and social contributions.

#### Germany:

  * **Vorabpauschale:** A pre-determined tax on investment income, applied annually. Ensures a minimum tax is paid even if no actual income is earned.

  * **Capital gains tax:** 25% flat rate, plus a solidarity surcharge and church tax (if applicable).

You can learn more about Capital gains tax and Vorabpauschale [here](<https://together.bunq.com/d/61600>).

#### Ireland:

  * **Deposit Interest Retention Tax (DIRT):** 33% tax on interest earned from deposits, including stock dividends.

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** 33% flat rate on profits from stock sales.

#### Italy:

  * [**Financial Transaction Tax (FTT):**](<https://together.bunq.com/d/60852>)**:** 0.1% tax on stock purchases on regulated markets, 0.2% for over-the-counter trades.

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** 26% flat rate on stock sale profits.

#### Luxembourg:

  * **Capital Gains Tax** : Exempt if held for >6 months and <10% ownership; otherwise taxed at income tax rates.

#### Netherlands:

  * **Box 3 tax:** Investment income, including capital gains from stocks, is taxed under Box 3. Instead of actual income, taxation is based on a deemed return on asset value. The deemed return is taxed at 31% (as of 2024).

#### Portugal: 

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** Generally taxed at a flat rate of 28%.

#### Slovakia: 

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** Exempt for shares held >1 year; otherwise taxed at 19–25%.

#### Spain:

  * [**Financial Transaction Tax (FTT):**](<https://together.bunq.com/d/60852>) 0.2% tax on the purchase of certain Spanish stocks.

  * **Capital Gains Tax:** Taxed at progressive rates, starting at 19% and rising to 23%, depending on the gain amount.

## Are my investments secured?

Your investments are securely stored in a **separate securities account** managed by **Upvest Securities** , a custodian regulated by the **German Federal Financial Supervisory Authority**.

This separate account ensures that your investments are legally safeguarded and kept distinct from the bank's own assets. This means that even in the unlikely event of the bank facing financial challenges or insolvency, your investments remain secure and unaffected. This setup provides a high level of protection for your investments.

## Why didn’t I get an instant payout from my Stocks sale? 

We strive to make life easy for you by making everything instant, including your Stock sales! 

It typically takes 2 business days for the Stock Market to process and finalize your Stock sale. To ensure that you get your payout instantly, we forward your money to you - up to €250,000 - even before the sale is completely processed by the Stock Market. 

If your stock sale exceeds €250,000, the amount will be paid out automatically once the stock market processes your sale. This should take up to 2 business days, which is why your payout might not be instant for larger transactions.
