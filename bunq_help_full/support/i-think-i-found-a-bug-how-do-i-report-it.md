# I think I found a bug. How do I report it?

**Source:** https://help.bunq.com/articles/i-think-i-found-a-bug-how-do-i-report-it
**Topics:** Support

---

**Hey bunqers 🌈,**

Spot a bug? 🔎 Thanks for letting us know!

The best way for you to report it would be through our [in-app chat](<https://go.bunq.com/link/support>) and use the “**Report a Bug** ” template from there. Then we'll work on a fix and we will update you as soon as possible 💪

### Do you want to report a bug from inside the bunq app?

If you encounter a bug in the bunq app, you can report it by simply taking a screenshot. A menu will appear, allowing you to report the bug.

You can also report a bug by following these steps:

  1. Take a screenshot of the bug in the app

  2. Tap on report

  3. Select **Report a bug**

  4. Describe the problem

  5. Press Submit

## Pro Tips: How to report a bug the best way possible?

  * **Check if the bug was reported already** To make your life easier we always post the most impactful reported bugs [here](<./most-impactful-reported-bugs>). We update all topics about bugs whenever we find a workaround or the bug is fixed. Keep an eye there to stay updated 🐛

  * **Screenshots and screen-recordings** We need to see what you see from your side on the screen. Every screenshot or screen-recording saves us time and helps us reproduce your bug faster. You can attach screenshots and screen-recordings through our Support chat, isn’t that cool? Even if you forget to attach a screenshot, don’t worry, our guide will instruct you to do so. 🏃‍♂️

  * **Detailed explanation** Every information helps us understand your case better. A bug can be born from different circumstances so that’s why we need as many details as possible. This can help us understand the reason why the bug occurred and will help us find a solution faster for you ✨

  * **Cooperation** Teamwork makes the dream work and when you report a bug you become a team with the support guide who’ll take over your case. When asked to provide certain information, screenshot or answer a question, the support guide requested this for a reason - to help you out as fast as they can. In most cases when asked a question from a guide this means that they need just a little more additional questions before they contact the responsible team for a solution 🔎
