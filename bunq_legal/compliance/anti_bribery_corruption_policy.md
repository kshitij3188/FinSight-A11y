fair when it comes to bribery and corruption. It’s here to make sure that nothing gets in

1. Introduction
At bunq, we do things the right way - no shortcuts, no shady deals. Most countries have
strict laws against bribery and corruption, and these rules don’t just stop at their borders.
They can apply to payments made to and from foreign entities, meaning breaking them -
even unintentionally - can lead to serious fines for businesses and even jail time for
individuals.
This policy makes it simple: follow the rules, no exceptions. If someone violates
anti-bribery laws, they face serious legal consequences, and at bunq, that also means
immediate dismissal.
Why does this matter for Eva? Because trust is everything. Eva expects bunq to be
transparent, ethical, and 100% focused on making her life easy. That means she never has
to worry about hidden risks, bad actors, or any behind-the-scenes nonsense that could
harm the bank she relies on. By staying clean and compliant, we keep her money safe and
her trust intact - so she can focus on what really matters: living life on her terms.
Legal Framework
bunq is all about making life easy, but we also play by the rules - no shady business here!
That’s why this policy is based on:
● Dutch Criminal Code (Wetboek van Strafrecht) – because bunq is a Dutch
company.
● US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act – applies to us since we're growing globally.
● UK Bribery Act – the UK is part of SEPA, so we need to stay compliant.
The US and UK laws have a wider reach and are stricter, which means they still apply to
bunq because:
1. Anyone (not just Dutch citizens) can become a bunq user.
2. We have big international ambitions.
3. The UK is in SEPA, making compliance a must.
While this policy sets the standard, stricter local laws always win - so if a country has
tougher rules, we follow them.

2. How to Act
Now that you know the basics of our Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy, let’s talk about
what this means for you at bunq. Simply put: we keep things clean, fair, and transparent -
no shady deals, no cutting corners. Here’s what that looks like in practice:
Facilitating payments
Facilitating payments are small payments made to speed up routine government
procedures, like obtaining permits, processing visas, clearing customs, securing police
protection, or accessing essential services such as utilities and cargo handling.
Even though some countries allow it, bunq does not. Facilitating payments - whether
using company money or personal funds - are strictly prohibited. Employees and any
hired government intermediaries must ensure that no such payments are made, even if
they are considered "normal" in certain regions. No undisclosed or unrecorded financial

Official government fees for faster service - like the €30 urgency fee for a passport in the

Conflicts of Interest
A conflict of interest happens when personal interests interfere with objective
decision-making at bunq. Employees must:
● Avoid situations that compromise their integrity or create risks for bunq.
● Disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest.
● Obtain written approval from bunq before engaging in external activities that
could pose a conflict.
For further details, refer to the Conflict of Interest Policy.
Social and Political Involvement
bunq’s involvement
bunq contributes to social and sustainability initiatives, but never in a way that could be
perceived as bribery or corruption. bunq’s charitable contributions and sponsorships must
never be:
● made to gain unfair advantages;
● made to individuals;
● and/or politically connected.
bunq’s charitable contributions and sponsorships must always be:
● permitted by local law;
● accurately recorded in bunq’s accounting system;
● for charitable or community purposes;
● and pre-approved by a member of the Managing Board.

The bunq employee responsible for dealing with a charity or similar organization/event
will always investigate the organization before any commitments (financial or otherwise)
are made. The investigation will include:
● an interview with one of the key representatives of the organization or other key
personnel (to the extent possible);
● a check whether any Government Officials are connected to the organization;
● a review of whether the organization is connected to or involved with any activities
in a High-Risk Country;
● a screening via public sources and the bunq due diligence tool such as the Dow
Jones tool;
● and a review of all facts relevant to determine whether a third party could perceive
the involvement with the organization as an intention of Bribery or Corruption.
The results of the investigation must be recorded and presented to the Compliance
Officer.
Political Activities
bunq does not participate in political activities, whether through direct or indirect
contributions. This includes payments to political parties, politicians, or political
candidates. Employees may not use company funds to pay for political event admission,
conferences, or fundraisers.
Employees are free to engage in political or social activities in their personal capacity, as
long as they:
● Act as private individuals, not representatives of bunq.
● Do not use company funds, resources, or time for these activities.
● Inform bunq by submitting an Employee Involvement Form if their involvement
could be perceived as linked to the company.
Gifts and Hospitalities
Employees must not accept or provide gifts, cash, or hospitality in exchange for
preferential treatment. This applies whether the offer comes directly from a business

3. Preventative Measures
bunq takes a proactive approach to prevent bribery and corruption. This means setting
high standards, doing proper checks before entering into partnerships, hiring with
integrity, and making sure everyone at bunq understands their responsibilities.
Dealing with Business Partners and Government Intermediaries
bunq expects all Business Partners to uphold the same ethical standards we do. To ensure
this and to avoid liability for corrupt practices - employees responsible for these
relationships must conduct a risk-based investigation before starting and throughout the
partnership. This is especially important in High-Risk Countries.
Business Partners are chosen for their expertise and ability to contribute to bunq’s
success, but significant payments made to them can present risks. That’s why thorough
due diligence is required. Depending on the level of risk, this may include:
● Checking public sources for reputation and integrity concerns;
● Identifying any links to Government Officials;

● Reviewing available business references;
● Conducting interviews with key representatives;
● Screening via bunq’s due diligence tool (e.g., Dow Jones tool);
● Assessing whether the Business Partner operates in a High-Risk Country;
● Looking out for red flags, such as unusually high commissions.
If the Business Partner is also a bunq user, some of these checks may have already been
completed during the customer acceptance process.
Government Intermediaries require extra caution. Relationships with government bodies
are particularly sensitive to bribery and corruption risks. Even if a Government
Intermediary follows local laws, their actions could still violate this Policy. For example, a
Government Intermediary making a Facilitating Payment - even where local law allows it -
is strictly prohibited. This Policy prevails over any local laws or regulations that are less
strict.
Recruitment
Integrity matters at bunq. That’s why all employees in integrity-sensitive positions are
screened before they start.
● Members of the Managing Board and Supervisory Board undergo screening by
Dutch regulatory authorities, such as De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB) and Autoriteit
Financiële Markten (AFM)
● Special care is taken when hiring family members of Government Officials, even if
the role is outside their home country. This can raise concerns, regardless of the
candidate’s qualifications. If such a situation arises, HR must consult the
Compliance Officer before moving forward
Training
Every employee is required to complete anti-bribery and corruption training as part of the
Code of Conduct training. This ensures a strong foundation in understanding and
applying these policies in day-to-day work.

4. Reporting (the Suspicion of) a Violation
Even with strong preventative measures, violations can still occur. If a Business Partner,
Government Official, or any other third party is suspected of violating this Policy, every
bunq employee has a responsibility to report it immediately to the Compliance Officer.
Failing to report could lead to legal and financial consequences for both bunq and the
individual.
Reporting Suspicious Activity
If you suspect that a bunq employee is involved in bribery or corruption—such as through
improper accounting—you must report it. Reports can be made to:
● The employee’s manager (if appropriate).
● The Compliance Officer (if reporting to a manager doesn’t seem suitable).
For more details on reporting concerns, refer to the Notification of Incidents Policy or the
Whistleblowing Policy.
bunq fully supports employees and Business Partners who refuse to give or receive bribes
and report any misconduct. No form of retaliation is tolerated against individuals who
report a concern in good faith—including suspected bribery or corruption.
If you believe reporting has led to or may lead to negative consequences, you can escalate
the matter directly to the Compliance Officer.
5. Non-adherence
Failing to follow this Policy has serious consequences, both within and beyond bunq.
Consequences at bunq
● For employees: Violating this Policy may result in severe disciplinary actions,
including suspension, demotion, or instant dismissal.
● For third parties: If a Business Partner, Government Official, or any other third party
is found to have breached this Policy, bunq may terminate all agreements with
them and, if necessary, pursue legal action, including criminal charges.

Legal and Reputational Consequences
Non-compliance doesn’t just affect bunq - it can have lasting consequences for
individuals and businesses involved, including:
● Civil, administrative, and criminal sanctions or liabilities.
● Reputational damage to employees, business partners, and bunq.
● Legal obligations to report violations to the relevant authorities, such as law
enforcement or regulatory bodies.
● bunq reserves the right to initiate legal proceedings separately from any regulatory
action.
6. Final Provisions
While every employee is responsible for following this Policy, the Compliance Officer
oversees enforcement. This includes monitoring compliance, assessing effectiveness, and,
in collaboration with relevant departments, updating the Policy and procedures as
needed.
For any questions regarding this Policy, please contact the Compliance Team.
