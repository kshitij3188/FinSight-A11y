1. Introduction
Rules. Everybody needs to have them, but banks even more so. Rules reflect our values
and help us maintain integrity. This document contains the Code of Conduct of bunq, to
which all employees must adhere.
The Code of Conduct (“Code of Conduct”) is intertwined with the Bankers' Oath and the
disciplinary law ("het bankentuchtrect”) of the Disciplinary Committee of the Dutch
Banking Association (“Tuchtrecht Banken”).
The present Code of Conduct puts these above-mentioned values into practice by
implementing them through more practical rules. The code of conduct describes the
basic principles that should control the behavior of each individual employee and act as a
guideline for how bunq should behave towards its user, partners, employees, and society
in general. The Code of Conduct formulates the general principles and rules under which
the employees of bunq perform their service duties.
In order to ensure that Adam operates with the highest level of integrity, bunq has
introduced Code of Conduct and Related Policies Training that every onboarding Adams
(including interns and contractors) must complete, covering Conflict of Interest policy,
Anti-Bribery and Corruption policy, Whistleblowing Procedure, Regulation Use and
Review of Communication Tools and Regulation private investment transactions. The
training must be completed by every new employee within 7 days of starting their journey
at bunq as Adam, and the rate of completion will be monitored by the compliance officer.
Ensuring that every employee at bunq will have the training completed and equipped
with appropriate understanding regarding proper conduct.
You are expected to live up to the Code of Conduct at all times, completely, without
exception. Not following them could have serious consequences, for bunq and for you.
Non-compliance with the Code of Conduct and its related policies can lead to disciplinary
and/or labor sanctions or a procedure at the Disciplinary Committee which can be
brought forward by any individual or organization living/working in the Netherlands.
If you are uncertain about anything regarding the Code of Conduct (and the internal rules
and regulations), check it with the Head of Compliance.

3. Code of Conduct
The Code of Conduct set out rules indicating desired and undesired behavior. As the
sphere we operate in is particularly sensitive to any sort of misconduct and mistakes, we
need to ensure that every single one of our bunqers is familiar with this Code of Conduct
and also with any consequences stemming from lack of compliance with them.
bunq has to deal with three different fields of interest in this context: those of the user, the
organization, and the individual employee. A balanced judgment between the various
interests must be made at any time.
bunq has drawn up a set of fifteen basic rules which can provide general guidance or
guidance in case multiple interests are involved and it might not be (immediately) clear
how to act. The premise is that the interests of a customer prevail over those of bunq
and/or that of an employee. In general, it can be said that the interests of the mass usually
prevail over that of an individual. An employee must also prevent that their actions, or any
omission thereof – also in the private domain – harm the interests of a user or bunq.

a. Treating each other with respect
We're a big family at bunq - with different cultures and Adams from all over the world. By
welcoming one another’s backgrounds and experiences, we become stronger together.
We work side by side, and diversity and inclusion are our strengths. To make it easy for you
to navigate life at bunq we have some ground rules to ensure a great environment where
all of us can thrive. Our ground rules can be seen as the baseline of our bunq culture and
concern how we communicate and treat each other. These are what keep the bunq family
intact, and are not to be taken lightly.
Our bunq family is all about respect, with an environment free from harassment and
discrimination. Put simply, we treat others like we want to be treated ourselves, kindly and
respectfully. We provide feedback based on the content and never on the person. In case
you ever feel you need to talk to someone and for whatever reason, you would like to talk
to someone else than your Team Lead, then reach out to HR or our company confidant -
whose name and contact can be found in Whistleblowing Procedure.
We'll take appropriate actions to prevent potential misconduct or in cooperating with an
investigation. At bunq, we treat each other with respect and we speak up for our fellow
Adams. We are here together and for each other!

orientation, gender identity, trade union membership or activity or current or past

g. Avoid conflicts of interest
A Conflict of Interest occurs if a personal interest or activity - family, friendships, financial,
or social factors - influences or appears to influence the ability of an individual to exercise
objectivity or impairs the individual’s ability to perform his or her employment
responsibilities at the workplace.
For instance, there would be some conflict of interest if:

i. Inside trading
Laws and Regulations prohibit the purchase or sale of securities by persons who are aware
of material nonpublic information about a company, as well as the disclosure of material,
nonpublic information about a company to others who then trade in the company's
securities.
This means that if, within the course of your mission with bunq, you deal directly with a
company that has listed Financial Instruments and have access to confidential
information about this company, you will be considered an insider and should refrain from
private trading (or advice to others) of financial instruments (e.g. exchange-traded shares,
bonds, options, futures, swaps, turbo's, sprinters, trackers, units in collective investment
schemes…) with these companies.
Private transactions with affiliates of bunq must be made in accordance with the
Regulation Private Investment Transactions and the Conflict of Interest Policy. If you have
any doubts about any deal, gift, or transaction you want to make with an affiliated
company, check back with the Head of Compliance to make sure that you are on the right
path.
j. Gifts and hospitalities
Suppliers, (prospective) partners, or users sometimes want to show their appreciation with
gifts and hospitality. However, to avoid the impression that they want something in return
or that this is a reward for granted favors, we have a gift ceiling of €100 per year per
(potential) Partner. Direct or indirect offers, payments, or acceptance of bribes in any form
are unacceptable.
Never accept gifts, invitations, or anything else of value that is provided in return for
preferential treatment, and never accept cash or loans, and vice-versa. e.g. If someone
offers you a Ferrari for preferential treatment, they are trying to bribe you. However, a box
of chocolates to share with your colleagues is a treat worth sharing.
