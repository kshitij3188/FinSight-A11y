- this policy has got you covered! It’ll walk you through what a Conflict of Interest is

not just their job - everyone at bunq, including IT and Customer Support, plays a role in
identifying and handling conflicts of interest.

3. When Can a Conflict Occur?
A Conflict of Interest may arise between:
1. bunq and a user (where the interests of bunq and the obligation it has towards a
user are inconsistent or contradictory);
2. Two or more users;
3. Employees/board members of bunq and users;
4. Employees/board members of bunq and bunq; and
5. The different activities within bunq.
Potential conflict of interest may arise when an employee:
● Engages in activities competing with bunq;
● Conducts business with bunq’s users or suppliers that could affect our reputation;
● Requests or accepts substantial favors from bunq for themselves or close relatives;
● Accepts entertainment offers from business relations that could lead to favoritism;
● Gives unjustified advantages to third parties at the expense of bunq;
● Exploits business opportunities of bunq for personal or family use;
● leverages bunq-related services or relationships for personal gain.
Or when an individual:
● Gains significant benefits for themselves or relatives due to their bunq position;
● Influences decisions at bunq for personal benefits; or
● Holds interests affecting their professional independence at bunq.

4. Preventative Measures in Place
At bunq, transparency and integrity come first—no hidden agendas, no shady deals. To
uphold these principles, several safeguards are in place to prevent conflicts of interest
before they arise. To keep things that way, we’ve got solid safeguards in place to prevent
conflicts of interest before they even start:
● Code of Conduct: outlines the principles of conduct, detailing guidelines on
additional occupations and receiving gifts to prevent conflicts;
● Regulation Private Investment Transactions: Establishes rules and procedures for
managing private investments and handling sensitive information, avoiding the
mix of personal and business interests;
● Technical and functional Separation: Departments within bunq are structured to
keep confidential information safe and prevent its transfer between teams;
● Remuneration policy: This policy allows bunq to manage risk by incentivizing
employees to keep the best interests of the company as priority;
● Onboarding Package: As part of their introduction to bunq, every new team
member familiarizes themselves with this policy through structured guidance and
training;
● Rules of the Managing and Supervisory board: These include provisions for
managing Conflicts of Interest, requiring annual declarations from board members
to ensure ongoing compliance;
● Annual Update of Conflict of Interest Statements and Positions: The Compliance
Team mandates that Management and Supervisory Board Members annually
declare their Conflicts of Interest, maintaining a focus on transparency and
integrity throughout the year.
With these measures in place, bunq ensures a fair and trustworthy banking experience
while staying aligned with its values.

5. How to Report Conflicts of Interest (and What Happens
Next)
If a (potential) conflict of interest arises that could significantly impact bunq or an
employee, it must be reported to the Compliance Team. Here’s the step-by-step process:
1. Declare It
Submit a confidential Conflict of Interest Declaration with key details:
● Who is involved (including close relatives, if relevant);
● A clear description of the situation.
2. Compliance Review
● A Compliance Officer will check everything;
● You’ll get a response on whether there’s an actual conflict and what needs
to happen next.
3. Next Steps (If a Conflict Exists)
What happens next depends on the situation:
● For users: Customer Support will let them know, and they can decide how
to proceed;
● For employees: Compliance will work with the relevant manager to sort it
out;
● For board members: Board rules apply (no shortcuts here!);
● For internal bunq matters: The managing board will step in;
● If Compliance is involved: A Legal Officer will take over to ensure fairness.
4. Keeping Things Fair for the Future
To prevent similar issues in the future:
● Safeguards will be implemented, such as restricting access to sensitive
information;
