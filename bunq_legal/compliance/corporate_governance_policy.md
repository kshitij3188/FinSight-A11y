1. Introduction
At bunq, we believe that transparency and good governance are the keys to building a
bank that puts Eva at the center. This Corporate Governance Statement outlines how we
comply with Articles 88 to 95 of the Capital Requirements Directive (CRD IV), as required
by Article 96 of CRD IV.
We keep things simple and clear: here, you’ll find how our management bodies are
structured, how we ensure accountability, and how we align bunq's strategy with the
interests of all our stakeholders. Our governance model guides us to grow sustainably, stay
resilient, and comply with all applicable laws and regulations, all while keeping Eva’s
needs front and center.
2. Our Regulatory Position
bunq does not qualify as a 'significant' bank under Article 6(4) of the SSM Regulation (No
1024/2013), nor do we benefit from any form of government intervention as outlined in
Article 93 of CRD IV.
We continue to operate independently, and ensure that our decisions reflect what’s best
for Eva, while upholding the same high standards of financial strength, risk management,
and governance as larger banks.
3. The Management Bodies
At bunq, clear roles and strong accountability are our cornerstones. We operate with a
two-tier board structure, consisting of:
● The Managing Board: responsible for the daily management of bunq;
● The Supervisory Board: independent from the Managing Board, providing
oversight and advice.
All members of both Boards meet the respective legal and regulatory requirements. They
have taken the Banking Oath, which is the financial sector oath or promise, and have
committed to act with integrity and professionalism. In their decision-making process,
both the Managing and Supervisory Boards take into account the interests of all
stakeholders: Eva, shareholders, employees, and society at large.

We ensure that all Board members commit sufficient time to their roles, empowering
them to perform their duties effectively. Clear internal rules and procedures guide the
operations of both Boards as well as the internal organization of bunq, ensuring that
everything runs smoothly and transparently.
4. Managing Board
The Managing Board leads bunq’s daily operations. It is responsible for setting and
executing our strategy, overseeing financial reporting, and ensuring strong risk
management by defining and monitoring bunq's risk appetite. The Managing Board is
composed of members who bring a wealth of expertise, knowledge, and experience,
which ensures that they perform their duties effectively. Given our strong focus on
technology, at least one member has extensive IT expertise, and at least one other
member holds deep knowledge of risk management.
We invest in continuous training and development for all Managing Board members,
ensuring that they are always equipped to fulfill their roles in a dynamic and rapidly
evolving world. When necessary, the members of the Managing Board may agree on a
division of duties. Any such agreements are shared with the Supervisory Board to ensure
full transparency.
5. The Supervisory Board
The Supervisory Board oversees the activities of the Managing Board and monitors the
overall direction of bunq. It also provides advice and support to the Managing Board when
needed. Certain key decisions by the Managing Board or the General Meeting of
Shareholders require prior approval from the Supervisory Board, adding an additional
layer of oversight and careful consideration.
bunq aims for a diverse and balanced composition of the Supervisory Board. Members are
selected for their ability to act independently, critically, and professionally in relation to
one another and the Managing Board. We invest in ongoing training for Supervisory
Board members to keep them informed about state-of-the-art developments relevant to
their roles and bunq’s business.

6. Recruitment
In expanding our Managing or Supervisory Boards, we adhere to a rigorous and
well-documented selection process. Each new appointment starts with a carefully crafted
function profile, ensuring that we attract candidates whose skills, experience, and mindset
align with bunq's values and needs. This approach ensures that every new Board member
enhances our ability to serve Eva, uphold regulatory standards, and contribute to bunq's
long-term success.
7. Remuneration
At bunq, our Remuneration Policy is designed to promote sound business practices and
responsible risk-taking, fully aligning with our risk appetite and long-term goals. Our
Remuneration Policy aims to:
● Prevent and discourage irresponsible risk-taking;
● Avoid conflicts of interest;
● Fairly and appropriately reward our team members based on their experience,
performance, and level of responsibility.
Our Remuneration Policy focuses on the long term, and ensures that personal objectives
align with bunq's broader interests, supports sustainable growth, and ensures effective
risk management. We review the policy annually, and any changes must be approved by
the Supervisory Board to keep it aligned with bunq’s risk framework and regulatory
obligations. Fixed remuneration is set fairly, reflecting the team member’s responsibilities
and contributions, as well as market developments.
We do not reward underperformance. Severance payments, where applicable, are only
made for services actually rendered and are calculated according to Dutch law (‘regels
voor transitievergoedingen’). The Managing Board manages the adoption,
implementation, and modification of our Remuneration Policy, with approval required
from the Supervisory Board. \The remuneration for the Supervisory Board itself must be
approved by the General Meeting of Shareholders.
