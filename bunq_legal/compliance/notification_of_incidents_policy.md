Summary
This policy describes bunq’s approach to internal notification of incidents. Section 1
introduces the motivation and legal background of this policy. Section 2 contains a table
of definitions which may be helpful while you read this policy. Section 3 describes the
process of notification of incidents, including reporting, investigation, and what happens
after. Section 4 contains additional remarks about when this policy may be amended and
how employees get to know this policy.

1. Introduction
As with any business, there’s always the chance something goes wrong. bunq’s goal is to
always provide our users with the best possible banking experience, but not all incidents
can be avoided. That’s why we made sure we’re always able to deal with incidents quickly
and efficiently. We have strong measures in place to identify (suspicions of) incidents and
subsequently solve any issues that may arise from them. Swift response to any issue is an
important part of keeping you and your money safe. How we deal with incidents is
explained in detail in this policy.
Legal Framework and Scope
bunq implemented this policy and the procedures mentioned in it pursuant to sections
3:10 and 3:17 of the Financial Supervision Act (“FSA”) and section 12 of the Decree
prudential rules FSA, which require bunq to have rules and procedures on handling and
registration of incidents. Furthermore, in the processes described in this policy, the
personal data of employees will be processed by bunq.

3. Procedure in case of incidents
Notification of incidents
Every bunq employee is responsible for reporting (suspicion of) incidents to the
Compliance Officer. The first task of the Compliance Officer is to make sure whether the
reported situation is indeed an incident. If this turns out to be the case, the Compliance
Officer will report the incident to the Managing Board and propose a plan to solve the
issue.
While the Managing Board decides what to do to solve the incident, the Compliance
Officer records the incident in the Incident Register:
● the nature of the Incident;
● the date and time when the Incident occurred;
● the department within bunq where the Incident occurred;
● the extent of the damage that the Incident caused;
● the date and time when the Incident was reported to the Compliance Officer; and
● if known, information about the employee(s) that have caused the Incident.
Note! Reports must follow two conditions: they must be reported internally and in good
faith. bunq does not tolerate any form of retaliation against reporting employees or
against those who report a concern in good faith. To report in good faith, in this case,
means to report only when you have reasonable grounds to believe the information was
true at the time of the reporting, even if the information turns out to be false. An
employee who disregards the internal process of reporting or does not report in good
faith will not be protected by this Policy.

If you, as an employee, suspect that another employee of bunq is involved in Bribery
and/or Corruption (for example, defective accounting) you must report this to the
manager of the employee or, if you don’t think that this would be appropriate, to the
Compliance Officer.
If an employee feels that their report may lead to or has led to any negative
consequences, they can report this concern to the Compliance Officer.
Anonymous notification of incidents
If the reporting employee does not feel comfortable revealing their identity, they may
report (the suspicion of) an incident anonymously. The Compliance Officer handles such
an anonymous report similar to the procedure described above, except the identity of the
employee is not revealed unless there is a legal obligation to disclose it.
Investigation of the incident
The Managing Board may decide to have the Compliance Officer or the Legal Officer
initiate an investigation into the incident. They may also decide to involve an external
advisor for an internal investigation. The person carrying out the investigation is
authorized to collect and review all information available at bunq, including the
information provided by employees of bunq.
Internal Investigation
Internal Investigations, which may involve monitoring and/or access to e-mail and/or
internet usage and/or communication tools of employees, are made by an independent
external advisor (such as a law firm or an accountant) appointed by the Managing Board.
An Internal Investigation may be initiated when (there is a strong suspicion that) an
incident has occurred related to fraud, abuse, and/or violation of laws and regulations
and/or internal rules and regulation of bunq. These are serious issues that bunq entrusts
to the independent external advisor in order to guarantee fair and efficient resolution of
(the suspicion of) an incident.
Reporting
The Compliance Officer reports the outcome of an investigation or makes sure that the
results of an Internal Investigation and the measures taken to mitigate the incident are
reported to the Managing Board. This also applies if a member of the Managing Board
first reported the incident.

If an employee caused an incident, they will be given a chance to defend themself before
the Compliance Officer reports to the Managing Board.
Measures to prevent incidents
bunq has measures in place to prevent incidents spread across policies and procedures of
bunq, such as (among others) the Information Security Policy, the Regulation Private
Investment Transactions, the Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy, and the Conflicts of
Interest Policy. If an incident still occurs despite the preventative measures, the Managing
Board or the Compliance Officer will, depending on the case and the result of an
Investigation or an Internal Investigation, take measures to mitigate the risks of the
incident and prevent the incident from happening again.
Disciplinary measures
Employees that caused incidents that may impact bunq, our employees, or our users will
face strict disciplinary measures. The measures are based on the corporate values of bunq,
bunq’s Code of Conduct, internal rules, and policies. The measures will be initiated by the
Managing Board and executed by Human Resources (HR). The employee will receive a
written notice of the disciplinary measures taken. The following measures may be used,
depending on the seriousness of actions or violations and the circumstances:
● a written warning;
● employee having to give up the advantages and/or profits they obtained from their
actions (which are the reasons they are facing disciplinary measures);
● a suspension (possibly combined with deduction of salary). The deduction will
happen only when the wrongdoing or negligence of the employee has been
reasonably demonstrated or proven;
● a discharge from their work position and/or a transfer to another department of
bunq;
● a combination of the above measures;
● instant dismissal from work at bunq; or
● other disciplinary measures.

If the actions of the employee that caused the incident qualify as a criminal offense, the
Managing Board may decide to press (criminal) charges.
The Compliance Officer will, after the conclusion of an investigation and/or an Internal
Investigation, register additional information in the Incident Register, including:
● the name(s) of the person(s) that has/have caused the incident (including their
position(s));
● the name(s) of the employee(s) that has/have reported the incident to the
Compliance Officer (unless the Incident was reported anonymously);
● the employee(s) (if any) that was/were familiar with the incident;
● the disciplinary measures imposed;
○ HR will also register these measures in the employee’s file.
● the measures taken to (i) mitigate the risks that occurred due to the Incident and
(ii) prevent the repetition of the Incident (or similar incidents).
All of these documents will be archived and confidential. Neither the Incident register nor
the employee files may be accessed by anyone other than the Compliance Officer (but
only to the extent which may be necessary for an investigation) and the HR, respectively.
Notification to a supervisory authority
If the Managing Board determines that an incident occurred, the Compliance Officer or
the Managing Board will report the incident to the Dutch Central Bank (“DNB”) as soon as
possible. The report will contain:
● the details, the facts and circumstances surrounding the Incident;
● the role and position of the employee(s) that has/have caused the Incident;
● the disciplinary measures imposed; and
● the measures taken to (i) mitigate the risks that occurred due to the Incident and
(ii) prevent the repetition of the Incident (or similar incidents).

4. Confidentiality and Protection
Confidentiality
All employees (and members of the Managing Board) involved in the execution of
processes described in this policy will act with the highest level of professional integrity
and confidentiality. Any document drafted following or pursuant to this policy will be
stored in a confidential manner and will be filed in the Incident Register. Only the
Compliance Officer has access to the Register and only HR has access to the employee's
file.
The investigation and/or Internal Investigation will be carried out diligently but in secret.
This is to prevent (i) speculation about the existence and extent of a possible incident
among employees and (ii) to avoid bringing unnecessary and unwanted attention to the
investigation and/or Internal Investigation. This approach is used to protect the privacy of
involved employees and, in case it is found that there was no incident, to not needlessly
disrupt the work of other employees.
Protection
An employee that follows the procedures described in this policy diligently and reports in
good faith will be protected from disadvantages that may be caused by their report such
as unfair treatment by the person they reported. This protection also applies to any
employee involved or questioned during an investigation and/or Internal Investigation.
