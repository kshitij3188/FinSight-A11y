Summary
Sometimes, things don’t go as they should. If bunq employees notice something that
feels off - something that could harm bunq, its people, or its users - they should feel safe
speaking up. This guide explains exactly how to report a concern, what happens next, and
the protections in place.
Section 1 outlines the purpose and importance of whistleblowing at bunq, explaining its
role in upholding integrity and the legal frameworks involved. The policy applies to
individuals who have taken the Bankers’ Oath.
Section 2 defines the types of concerns and issues that should be reported to help
maintain a safe and ethical environment.
Section 3 clarifies who can report concerns, including bunq employees, partners, and
those closely associated with bunq.
Section 4 provides a step-by-step guide for submitting reports, covering both internal and
external options.
Section 5 describes the process that follows after a report is made, detailing what
whistleblowers can expect in terms of procedures and timelines.
Section 6 emphasizes the protections and safeguards for whistleblowers, ensuring that
they are not subject to retaliation or negative consequences.

1. Introduction
Purpose and Principles
At bunq, we foster a culture of openness, accountability, and integrity - where everyone
feels comfortable asking for help or raising concerns. Speaking up isn’t just encouraged;
it’s a responsibility that comes with working at bunq. bunq employees commit to acting
with integrity by taking the Bankers’ Oath. This oath isn’t just a formality - it’s a
commitment to always do the right thing. It empowers employees to report any issues
they notice while also reminding them to stop, think, and ensure that their actions align
with bunq’s mission of serving users honestly and efficiently. For more on ethical
decision-making, check out our Code of Conduct.
To uphold these principles, bunq provides a clear and safe process for reporting concerns.
This procedure ensures that:
● Employees can report any (potential or suspected) wrongdoing internally to the
global or regional Confidant (see Section 4).
● Employees can report (potential or suspected) wrongdoing directly to an external
competent authority.
● Employees can speak up without fear of retaliation, discrimination, or unfair
treatment.
● Reports are handled with confidentiality, and whistleblowers - as well as the
employees they report - are protected as much as possible.
When employees raise a concern in good faith, bunq investigates and takes action if
necessary. Retaliation, intimidation, or punishment for speaking up is not tolerated -
whistleblowers are protected.
This procedure applies to bunq B.V., its branches, and subsidiaries (hereinafter “bunq”).
Local branches or subsidiaries may have additional guidelines to comply with local laws
and regulations.
Legal framework
This procedure aligns with:

2. What Can be Reported as ‘Wrongdoing’
Wrongdoing? That’s when someone does something shady - like breaking the rules,
acting immorally, or just not acting when they should - and it ends up messing with the
public interest. The Dutch Whistleblowers Authority Act (Article 1d) calls it ‘misstand,’ but
let’s keep it simple: it’s about doing the right thing and calling out what’s not, which
includes but is not limited to:
● Violation of European Law;
● Violation of civil law, criminal law, or other statutory requirements;
● Violation of Wwft1 (internal report);
● Violations of bunq’s internal rules in ways that affect the public interest - like
deliberately hiding, destroying, or manipulating information;
● Any misconduct that goes against bunq’s Code of Conduct, including harassment,
bullying, and discrimination;
● Actions that violate the Bankers’ Oath, such as lack of transparency, failing duty of
care, or sharing confidential information;
● Any behavior that threatens the stability and continuity of the financial system;
● Acts or omissions that put public health, personal safety, or the environment at
risk;

3. Who Can Report a Wrongdoing ?
This procedure applies to anyone who works (or has worked) for bunq, no matter their
role, contract type, or location. This includes:
● Employees (current and former);
● Directors and officers;
● Full-time, part-time, and temporary staff;
● Freelancers and contractors;
● Interns and trainees;
● Workers employed through an intermediary partner.
If someone raises a concern in good faith and in the context of their work at bunq, they
are considered a Whistleblower and are protected under applicable laws, regulations, and
this procedure.
4. How to Report a Wrongdoing ?
Whistleblowing reports can be submitted through internal or external reporting channels.
Reports can also be made anonymously.
Internal reporting channel
Reporting in Person
A wrongdoing can be reported by requesting a one-on-one conversation with a global or
regional Confidant.
Reporting in Writing
Reports can be submitted by: ending an encrypted email to whistleblower@bunq.com;

All reports are received by trusted Confidants, who are appointed by the Managing Board.
Confidants handle reports confidentially, assess the concerns raised, and ensure proper
investigation.
Exceptional circumstances
If the concern involves:
- A Management Board Member, it must be reported to the Supervisory Board;
- A Supervisory Board Member, it must be reported to the Chairman of the
Supervisory Board;
- A Confidant, concerns can be reported directly to the Management Board or
Chairman of the Supervisory Board.
Competent External Authority
Whistleblowers have the right to report externally without using internal channels first.
Reports can be submitted to competent Dutch external authorities as listed in Article 2c of
the Dutch Whistleblowers Protection Act. Reports can be made:
1. by writing;
2. orally via phone or other voice messaging systems, or
3. In person (by requesting a meeting within a reasonable time frame).
For Violations of the Bankers’ Oath
If the concern relates to a violation of the Bankers’ Oath, a report can be submitted to
Tuchtrecht Banken, the independent Dutch banking disciplinary body. Reports can be
made regarding:
● Lack of care and integrity;
● Failing to balance interests properly;
● Not putting the user first;
● Violating laws or regulations;
● Sharing confidential information;

Tracking Reports for Transparency
The number of reports submitted to bunq’s Confidants can highlight potential challenges

Key principles when filing the report
When submitting a report, two key principles apply:
1. Stick to the facts – Reports should be based on verifiable information, not
speculation;
2. Act in good faith – Concerns should be raised with the right intentions, not for
personal grievances.
Keep it honest, keep it clear!
1. Providing Clear and Useful Information
To properly investigate a concern, reports should include accurate and complete
information. That might mean providing documents as evidence or, at the very least,
having reasonable grounds to believe something is wrong.
bunq may choose not to investigate if:
● The report doesn’t have enough details or supporting evidence;
● It’s not possible to gather more information (especially for anonymous reports).
2. Good Faith Reporting
Concerns raised honestly and in good faith - even if the information turns out to be
incorrect - are always protected.

5. What Happens Next ?
Reporting wrongdoing takes courage, and it’s taken seriously. Every report is handled
fairly, confidentially, and in a timely manner. Here’s what happens after a report is
submitted:
Receipt of Report
A confirmation of receipt will be sent within seven days. A designated person or team will
take responsibility for assessing and investigating the concern.
Recording
If a report is made by phone or in a live conversation, it will either:
● Be recorded (only with prior consent), or
● Be documented in a written record.
All reports are stored in a strictly confidential case management system. Only the
Confidant or a designated officer will have access to the Whistleblower’s identity.
Investigation and assessment
The Confidant will review the concern using the information provided, along with any
necessary interviews or additional research. If needed, external experts may be consulted
to ensure a full understanding of the situation.
The Confidant will then report their findings and recommend follow-up actions to the
CEO, with a copy to the CRO. If the concern involves a Managing Board Member, the
report will go directly to the Chairman of the Supervisory Board.
Updates
Staying informed is important. The Confidant will provide regular updates on the status of
the report. A substantial response will be shared within three months of the report being
made.

6. How Can You Be Protected as a Whistleblower ?
Speaking up should never feel like a risk. Employees who report wrongdoing in good faith
are protected from any form of retaliation.
Zero tolerance for retaliation
Retaliation can take many forms, including:
● Dismissal, suspension, or demotion;
● Withholding promotions or giving a negative review;
● Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion;
● Public defamation, contract termination, or revocation of a license.
bunq does not tolerate retaliation against Whistleblowers who report wrongdoing in good
faith. Anyone who attempts to retaliate will face consequences, including disciplinary
action, civil action, or criminal prosecution.
The Compliance Officer for Regulatory Compliance oversees the whistleblowing process
to ensure no whistleblower is disadvantaged due to their report.
Confidentiality
Any information related to:
● A report of wrongdoing;
● An investigation into wrongdoing;
● The results of an investigation;
● Any actions taken as a result
…is considered strictly confidential.
Information will only be shared on a need-to-know basis. The Whistleblower’s identity will
be protected, and their name will never be disclosed without prior written permission
from the Managing Board or if required by law.

Anonymity
Whistleblowers have the right to remain anonymous. To do so, reports must be submitted
through a confidential intermediary, such as a lawyer. Even in these cases, the
Whistleblower’s identity remains protected.
Privacy and Data Protection
The Whistleblowing process complies with the General Data Protection Regulation
(GDPR) to ensure that both the Whistleblower and the reported person’s personal data
are protected.
Personal data is processed strictly for:
● Conducting investigations and taking necessary action;
● Meeting legal obligations;
● Improving this Whistleblowing Procedure;
● Keeping accurate records.
All personal data collected is handled in line with our internal documents and applicable
privacy regulations.
