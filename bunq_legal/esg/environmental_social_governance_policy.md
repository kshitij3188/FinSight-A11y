5.1 Our Approach to Operating Sustainably
At bunq, sustainability isn’t a side project — it’s part of who we are. From the start, we’ve
built bunq as a neo-bank: fully digital, borderless, and branch-free. This model isn’t just more
efficient — it’s better for the planet.
Because we don’t operate physical branches, our Scope 1 and 2 emissions are extremely
low compared to traditional banks. No heating and cooling of big buildings. No rows of
empty desks. No energy-hungry infrastructure. That means:
● Fewer greenhouse gas emissions;
● Lower energy use;
● Cleaner air for everyone.
This isn’t just operational efficiency — it’s a future-proof model that helps fight climate
change, every day.
When we choose not to occupy real estate, we leave space for something better:
● Local shops instead of sterile bank branches;
● Homes for families instead of vacant corporate real estate;

5.3 Climate-Related Risks
Climate risk isn’t just theoretical — it’s already affecting how banks operate. At bunq, we’ve
embedded Climate & Environmental (C&E) risk into our core strategy. Why? Because
financial resilience and environmental responsibility go hand in hand.
Our Method: 7 Steps
1. Identifying C&E Risks: Assessing physical risks (like floods, droughts, and rising sea
levels) and transition risks (such as policy changes and technological advancements).
2. Risk Type Selection: Integrating C&E risks into our existing risk categories, focusing on
credit, market, liquidity, operational, and strategic risks.
3. Portfolio & Sector Analysis: Analyzing investment portfolios and key sectors to
pinpoint vulnerabilities.
4. Assessing Likelihood and Impact: Evaluating the likelihood and potential impact of
these risks over short-term (less than 3 years), medium-term (3-5 years), and long-term
(over 5 years) horizons.
5. Mapping Transmission Channels: Understanding how these risks can affect our
financial activities.
6. Impact Measurement: Conducting in-depth analyses to determine potential financial
consequences.
7. Heatmaps: Creating visual heatmaps to highlight significant risks and prioritize efforts.

Key Insights from Our Assessment
Our comprehensive assessment revealed critical insights into specific risks:
● Physical Risks:
○ Extreme Weather Events: Flooding, storms, and heatwaves pose significant
threats to our mortgage portfolios, particularly in regions prone to such events.
For instance, properties in low-lying areas of the Netherlands are at a higher risk
of flooding, which could lead to increased loan defaults.
○ Water Scarcity: This risk is particularly relevant for agricultural loans, as regions
dependent on consistent water supply may experience reduced crop yields,
affecting borrowers' ability to repay loans.
○ Biodiversity Loss: Areas experiencing significant biodiversity decline could see
decreased property values, impacting the collateral for loans and increasing the
likelihood of financial loss.
● Transition Risks:
○ Policy Changes: New environmental regulations, such as stricter emission
standards, can affect the financial health of borrowers, especially in heavy
industries. For example, companies in the automotive and manufacturing
sectors might face increased compliance costs, which could impact their
creditworthiness.
○ Technological Shifts: The transition to greener technologies can disrupt
traditional industries, affecting loan repayments from businesses that are slow
to adapt. This is particularly pertinent in sectors like energy and manufacturing.
○ Market Preferences: Increasing consumer demand for sustainable products
can shift market dynamics, influencing the strategic positioning of our
investments. Businesses that fail to meet these changing preferences may
struggle financially.
● Sector and Location Focus:
○ High-Risk Sectors: Real estate, agriculture, and manufacturing sectors were
identified as particularly vulnerable to both physical and transition risks. These
sectors are crucial to our lending and investment strategies, necessitating
detailed risk management plans.
○ Geographic Focus: The Netherlands and Ireland are key locations where our
portfolios are most exposed. For example, coastal areas in these countries face
higher risks of flooding and sea level rise, while industrial regions may be more
affected by transition risks such as policy changes.
Stress Testing
We ensure resilience through rigorous stress tests:
● Scenario Analysis: Using scenarios like "Current Policies" and "Net Zero 2050" to
understand the potential impacts of severe physical and transition risks. These
scenarios help us anticipate significant future changes and prepare accordingly.
● Portfolio Impact: Stress tests reveal how extreme conditions, such as major floods or
rapid policy shifts, could impact our key portfolios. For instance, we analyze how a
significant rise in sea levels could affect the value of our real estate assets and increase
loan defaults in affected areas.

Rooted in International Standards
Our human rights approach is grounded in globally recognized frameworks, including:
● The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR);
● UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs);
● OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises;
● UN Global Compact Principles;
● UN Principles for Responsible Banking (PRB);
● UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
These commitments go beyond legal compliance — they reflect bunq’s belief in fair, ethical,
and sustainable business practices.
UN Principles for Responsible Banking (PRB): These principles are designed to integrate
sustainability into banks’ strategies and activities. By committing to the UN PRB, we align our
business practices with principles that go beyond regulatory compliance, emphasizing
corporate social responsibility (CSR) in our decisions. This commitment ensures that our
banking practices support global sustainability goals and the achievement of the UN SDGs.
Human Rights and Business: UN's Guiding Principles
In June 2011, the UN Human Rights Council adopted the Guiding Principles on Business &
Human Rights (UNGPs). These principles provide guidelines for countries and businesses,
emphasizing the obligation of states to protect human rights and the responsibility of
businesses to respect them. Companies commit to use leverage to remedy or compensate
for adverse impacts on human rights. The UNGPs also affirm the need to engage with
affected stakeholders and ensure that individuals, including human rights defenders who
raise concerns, are protected from retaliation or intimidation. Our adherence to these
principles reflects our dedication to prioritizing human rights in all our operations.
OECD Guidelines and UN Global Compact
We follow the OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises, which help shape our human
rights, labor, and ethical conduct policies. These guidelines, adopted by all industrialized
OECD member countries, are implemented globally through National Contact Points (NCPs).
We also support all ten principles of the UN Global Compact, recognizing the deep
connection between human rights, the environment, and anti-corruption.
Promoting Stakeholder Well-being
We are committed to the well-being, rights, and protection of our employees by adhering to
the ILO Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work. This includes:
● Freedom of Association and Collective Bargaining: Upholding the right of
employees to freely associate and engage in collective bargaining.
● Elimination of Forced and Child Labor: Ensuring that our operations and limited
supply chains are, and continue to be free from any form of forced or child labor.
● Preventing Human Trafficking: We are committed to supporting the fight against
human trafficking. We remain vigilant and responsive to any risks of human
trafficking within our operations and supply chain, aiming to contribute to global
efforts against this unacceptable violation of human rights.

bunq respects the rights of Indigenous Peoples, and other affected communities, as set out
in the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP). We
