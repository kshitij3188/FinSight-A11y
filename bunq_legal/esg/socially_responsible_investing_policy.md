1 Introduction
At bunq, we believe doing good shouldn’t be complicated. That’s why our mission goes
beyond creating seamless banking experiences – it’s about doing what’s right for our users
and the planet we all share.
Since 2019, our Socially Responsible Investing (SRI) Policy has helped guide where we put
your money, making sure we do no significant harm. That means avoiding investments that
go against our values – like polluting the environment, harming people, or violating basic
human rights.
We carefully select investment opportunities that align with our commitment to a better
world. Below you’ll find how we put this into action when it comes to companies we invest
in.
2 SRI for Companies
When bunq considers investing in a company, we go beyond financial metrics. We look at
how a company performs on Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) issues, and
whether it’s involved in any controversial activities.
This approach helps us manage non-financial risks (like reputational risks) while staying
aligned with international standards, including:
● UN Principles for Responsible Investing
● UN Global Compact & Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights
● OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises
We expect companies we invest in to implement human rights due diligence processes in
line with the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, including mechanisms
for grievance handling and remedy where adverse impacts occur.
bunq does not finance, or invest in, companies that are primarily active in:
● Fossil fuel-fired power
○ Exclusion Details: Investments are prohibited in companies that are involved
in the extraction or production of fossil fuels. This includes but is not limited to
coal (thermal & metallurgical), oil, food & feed based biofuels and natural gas.
This also includes companies engaged in new oil and gas exploration and
development, coal fired power plants, as well as companies active in the
extraction of oil from tar sands. This policy extends to companies directly
involved in these sectors, as well as those indirectly involved through
significant supply chain contributions or ownership stakes in businesses
engaged in these activities. In addition, we exclude the investments in
industrial production of electricity using fossil fuel resources.
○ Companies are also excluded if they are active in oil and gasfired power
generation for more than 30% of their electricity generated.
○ Rationale: Fossil fuels are the largest source of anthropogenic greenhouse gas
emissions, which significantly contribute to global warming and

bunq does not finance, or invest in, companies which are active in:
● Tobacco and Narcotics1
○ Exclusion Details: Companies primarily involved in the production, wholesale,
or retail distribution of tobacco or narcotic products are excluded.
○ Rationale: Tobacco and narcotics present significant public health risks,
contributing to widespread health issues and social problems. Avoiding
investment in these sectors reflects bunq’s commitment to promoting health
and well-being.
● Mining Activities
○ General Exclusion: Investments in the broader mining sector are prohibited
due to the significant environmental and social impacts associated with
mining operations. This overarching exclusion reflects bunq's commitment to
environmental conservation, climate change mitigation, and social
responsibility, ensuring that investments do not support practices that lead to
environmental degradation, biodiversity loss, social disruption, or human
rights violations.
○ Specific Exclusions:
Mountaintop Mining
■ Exclusion Details: Investments are prohibited in companies involved in
mountaintop removal mining, which involves removing the summit or
summit ridge of a mountain to access underlying coal seams.
■ Rationale: This method leads to extensive environmental damage,
including deforestation and loss of biodiversity, severely impacting
local ecosystems and water quality. The destruction of habitats disrupts
biodiversity and increases carbon emissions, directly conflicting with
global climate goals and bunq's commitment to sustainability and
ecological responsibility.
Asbestos Mining
■ Exclusion Details: No investments are made in companies engaged in
asbestos mining, due to the extreme health risks associated with
asbestos exposure and the environmental impact of mining
operations.
■ Rationale: The health risks, including serious lung diseases and cancers
linked to asbestos, alongside the environmental degradation caused by
mining operations, underscore the necessity of this exclusion for
protecting public health and preserving local ecosystems.
Development of New & Expanding Coal Mines
■ Exclusion Details: Investments in companies that develop new coal
mines (thermal & metallurgical) are strictly prohibited.
■ Rationale: New coal mining activities not only exacerbate climate
change by increasing greenhouse gas emissions but also lead to
significant biodiversity loss. The development of new mines often
involves clearing large areas of land, destroying habitats and leading to
loss of species. This practice undermines global efforts to combat

environmental preservation guides our exclusion of such investments.

significant ethical and animal welfare concerns. These practices often lead to
animal suffering, violate natural behaviors, and can result in ecological
imbalances. Excluding such investments aligns with bunq's commitment to
ethical standards, promoting animal welfare and preserving biodiversity. This
approach ensures our investments reflect our dedication to respecting and
protecting animal life and natural habitats.

3 SRI for Countries
At bunq, we believe in a world where countries lead with freedom, fairness, and care for the
planet. That’s why we apply the same socially responsible standards to investing in countries
as we do to companies.
We only invest in countries that respect political freedom, fight corruption, and take action
to protect the environment. If a country fails to meet at least two out of these three
principles, we won’t invest. Simple as that.
1. Exclusion of Countries with Violations of Basic Political Freedoms and Civil Rights
● Indicator: Freedom in the World published by Freedom House, classifying countries
as ‘Free’, ‘Partly Free’, or ‘Not Free’.
● Exclusion Level: Countries classified as ‘Not Free’ by Freedom House.
2. Exclusion of Countries Considered Highly Corrupt
● Indicator: Corruption Perception Index published by Transparency International (TI),
ranking countries from 100 (very little corruption) to 0 (very high corruption).
● Exclusion Level: Countries with a CPI score of less than 30.
3. Exclusion of Countries with Poor Environmental Performance
● Indicator: SDG Index published by the Sustainable Development Solutions Network
(SDSN) and the Bertelsmann Stiftung, assessing countries' performance on
Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
● Exclusion Level: Countries with an average score for SDGs 7 (Affordable and Clean
Energy), 13 (Climate Action), 14 (Life below Water), and 15 (Life on Land) of less than 50.

4 Important Considerations
A Little Room for Flexibility
We take our Socially Responsible Investing Policy seriously — but we also know that some
investments are a bit more complex. That’s why we allow a small exception:
● Up to 5% of our balance sheet can be invested in products that don’t fully meet
our SRI criteria.
This mainly applies to things like Collateralized Loan Obligations (CLOs) and investment
funds that include a wide range of entities. Because of their structure, it's not always possible
to screen every entity in advance.
We only allow this exception where necessary, and it helps us stay financially smart while
keeping our overall impact aligned with our values.
Speaking Up, Carefully
When we engage with companies or countries that aren’t living up to ESG expectations, we
usually do so privately. But if it helps move things forward, we may communicate externally
to push for change. We’re always thoughtful and discreet — especially when it involves
sensitive or confidential situations.
Engagement First, Exclusion If Needed
Our first step is always to engage. If an investment raises ESG concerns, we:

Amendments - May 2025
As part of our commitment to responsible investing, bunq has strengthened its Socially
Responsible Investment (SRI) policy to reflect evolving global standards and deepen our
environmental and ethical commitments.
To align more closely with a 1.5°C climate scenario, we now exclude companies that generate
over 30% of their electricity from oil- or gas-fired power and have added specific exclusions
for thermal and metallurgical coal, tar sands, and expanding fossil fuel projects. We’ve also
expanded our animal welfare criteria by excluding companies involved in the production,
manufacture, or sale of fur and exotic leather — supporting more humane and sustainable
practices. In the arms sector, we now explicitly exclude companies involved in incendiary
weapons, lethal autonomous weapons systems, and dual-use goods with non-civilian
purposes, ensuring alignment with international humanitarian standards.
We have also updated our list of excluded countries to reflect the most recent assessments
of governance, human rights, and transparency. As such, Angola, Burundi, Cambodia,
Nicaragua, Niger, and Uzbekistan have been added to our exclusion list, while Djibouti has
been removed as per the criteria.
These updates reflect our ongoing effort to promote sustainability, ethics, and transparency
in every investment decision, as we work toward a greener and more responsible future.
