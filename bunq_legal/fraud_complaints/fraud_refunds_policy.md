Summary
This policy describes bunq’s approach to fraud refunds. After reading this policy you
should have a clear idea of what we do to prevent fraud and what are the conditions for
getting a refund for unauthorized payments related to different incidents such as
phishing, spoofing, unauthorized card payments, and phone payments. After getting to
know our policy, the final section will explain how you can request a refund via the bunq
App!
● Sections 1 and 2 contain background information.
● Section 3 presents a list of measures bunq has against phishing scams.
● Section 4 describes the conditions that must be met by users requesting a refund
when they experienced phishing or spoofing.
● Section 5 describes the conditions that must be met by users that request a
refund for unauthorized card payments, making a distinction between payments
that require a pin and those that don’t.
● Section 6 describes the conditions that must be met by users that request a
refund for mobile payments.
● Section 7 lays out steps on how to request a refund via our app and where to
report a phishing incident.
This policy touches on the complicated topic of gross negligence. To make it easy to
understand how it applies to the topic at hand we included a lot of examples of what can
be considered gross negligence, in white boxes below. Keep in mind: Those examples are
non-exhaustive!

1. Introduction
Did you notice an unauthorized transaction from your account? Do not worry, we are here
to help! This policy is here to explain what happened to you, how (and when) you can
apply for a refund, and what conditions you must meet. For instructions on how to
request a refund please refer to Section 7 of this policy.
2. Definitions: Unauthorized Payment and Gross Negligence
What is an “Unauthorized payment”?
An unauthorized payment is a payment that occurred outside of your control - possibly as
a result of phishing, spoofing, or someone getting access to your payment instrument
(card, phone, password used to give monetary payments online).
Banks can only execute a transaction when the user has authorized the transaction. With
the bunq App and bunq Web, you can give us payment orders. We will only execute
orders after you have approved them via our app, authorizing the payment by doing so. To
approve an order, you must log in to the app and press the approval button that pops up.
For security reasons, we will sometimes ask you to confirm your approval with an extra
action, for example, by entering a code or through biometric authentication. This process
is described further in our Terms and Conditions (Section 15).
If you did not authorize a payment with this process but you see that a transaction
happened, you may have experienced an unauthorized transaction.
Largely, there are two factors that can prevent you from getting a refund for an
unauthorized payment. First, acting fraudulently or being complicit in fraud will strip you
of your right to a refund. Second, being grossly negligent can prevent you from having a
right to a refund, which can manifest in your not following our Terms & Conditions or not
informing us on time when you discovered an issue.
What is “gross negligence”?
The concept of gross negligence is tricky. Dutch law does not provide a clear definition,
but case law states that gross negligence can be seen as ‘not acting when a danger was
connected to the negligence, the danger of which the User was aware of and yet did not
lead the User to act differently’. Basically, you encountered something suspicious or

dangerous but did not act on it, meaning you were extremely (grossly) negligent in your
actions. A level of carelessness has to be present.
However, whether gross negligence is present in a case always depends on the
circumstances. Whether or not a person was grossly negligent has to be assessed against
what can be expected of a “reasonably informed and reasonably observant payment
service user”.
NOTE: Businesses tend to be more professional and tend to employ people specialized in
their respective fields. Given this fact, business users must follow a higher standard of care
than a “reasonably informed and observant payment service user”.
What is phishing?
Phishing involves the fraudster obtaining a user’s credentials, which is usually done via a
fake payment request or bunq web login page, and making payments using the user’s
account.
What is spoofing?
Spoofing occurs when a fraudster pretends to be something or somebody else, such as
bunq, the user’s friend, or the tax authorities (Belastingdienst). This can lead to a payment
by the victim to the fraudster or to the victim relinquishing their login credentials to the
fraudster, after which the fraudster makes the payments themselves (which means that
the spoofing was used as a prelude to phishing). In this policy, we only touch on situations
where the victim gives away their credentials and the fraudster makes the payments.
Cases, where the victim transferred the money to the fraudster, are outside the scope of
this policy since they are not deemed to be “unauthorized”.
3. Existing measures against phishing

such as:
● Reporting to telecommunication companies: We report senders of phishing SMS
to telecommunication companies and they block the senders.
● Together topics: On our Together portal we regularly make posts informing and
warning our Evas about phishing.

4.2 Phishing refunds
You can get a refund when you were phished if you:
1. present a credible story to us about what happened (supported with a police
report);
2. have not acted fraudulently or are complicit in fraud; and
3. have not acted with gross negligence.
NOTE: To determine if you are involved in fraud is more straightforward than to determine
if you acted with gross negligence. Thus, here are some examples of what you could have
done that can amount to gross negligence:
● You checked your bank account within two weeks (for business users: one week)
after the unauthorized transactions, but did not notify bunq immediately.
● You notified bunq more than two weeks (for business users: one week) after the
unauthorized transactions and have no credible explanation for why you did not
check your bank account sooner.
● The fraudsters called you and, if you are a personal user, we have warned you (via a
message, such as emails or notifications) within the last six months of the fact that
we never call.
● You were called by the fraudsters who did not impersonate bunq, but instead
impersonated someone else (relative, Microsoft, police, etc.) and got hacked as a
result of it.
● You have not cooperated at all or sufficiently with our investigation.
● You still had access to the account after a suspicion of fraud appeared but did not
act on it (notifying us, changing security codes).
● You did not act upon our warnings that a new device has been registered (while
you were still able to, for instance, change your security codes and/or remove
devices). When a new device is added to your account, you will receive an email
notification stating that a new device is added. We assume and expect you to

4.3 Spoofing refunds
To get a refund in case of spoofing, you must:
1. be a personal user;
a. Business users must adhere to a higher standard;
2. Be an active bunq user in a good standing with us (meaning you are not and never
were suspected/involved in fraudulent behavior);
3. present a credible story to us about what happened (supported with a police
report);
4. not act fraudulently or be complicit in fraud, and
5. not act with gross negligence.
6. It was impossible, or at least very hard, for you to have noticed the fraud.
NOTE: To be able to get a refund, you need to reach out to us. We will not contact users
about possible refunds!

5.1 Mastercard and Maestro Card Refund.
To qualify for a refund of an unauthorized payment from a Mastercard/Maestro card, you
must:
1. provide us with a credible story on what happened.
2. provide us with a police report.
3. not act fraudulently or be complicit in fraud.
4. not, intentionally or with gross negligence, breach the terms & conditions of bunq.
Similarly to refunds for phishing or spoofing, you lose the right to a refund if you
intentionally or with gross negligence neglected to follow the terms and conditions in
using the payment instrument or did not inform bunq of the loss, theft, unlawful or
unauthorized usage immediately upon discovering the said loss, theft, or usage.
Here are examples of how terms & conditions may be breached with gross negligence:
● keep your login codes and other security features strictly to yourself, do not share
them with anyone else and never use them anywhere but in our official apps or in
our official web interface;
● make sure that your cards are not used by anyone else but you
● check your account at least once every two weeks
● when using the bunq app or web interface in a public place, look over your
shoulder to make sure that no unauthorized person is peeking;
● keep your card in a secure place and check frequently if you still have it;
● don’t store your PIN or any other secure number(s) in a way (physically or digitally)
that allows them to be used by others;
● make sure no one is watching when you enter the PIN or other secure numbers;
● When you know or suspect that your card is stolen, broken, lost, or in any other way
misused, please immediately block the card via the bunq app and report this to us
right away;
Here are some examples of gross negligence related to Maestro/Mastercard payments:

5.2 Pinless payments.
Pinless payments can go up to €50,- per payment, and up to €100,- in total per day. Above
these amounts, a pin is required.
To qualify for a refund of an unauthorized pinless payment you must:
1. provide us with a credible story on what happened;
2. request a refund for a payment that was <€50,-;
If the payment is above €50,- a pin should be required
3. Request a refund for the first payment made without a pin;
To be clear, we will only refund the first payment made without a pin being required. In
case of further payments, you should have been aware of your card or phone being stolen
or misused.
4. not act fraudulently;
The right to a refund of an unauthorized pinless payment is lost if you acted fraudulently.
An example of fraud would be if the user acts with the intention of misleading bunq.
bunq can only set detection methods. Detection does not guarantee that all fraudulent
transactions will be detected or that the fraud will always be prevented.
5. inform us as soon as possible;
Whether a user has notified bunq soon enough depends on the circumstances of the
case. If a user noticed a payment or should have noticed a payment (e.g. we inform the
user about a payment that we noticed), they should inform us immediately afterward.
Special cases
Exceptions to these refund rules are when (i) the user is deemed to be complicit in the
fraud, (ii) the user has already received a refund earlier (for another phishing/spoofing
case) or (iii) the user does not cooperate sufficiently with the investigation.

6. Mobile phone payments
Unauthorized payments made via a mobile phone are assessed the same way as card
payments, as it is considered a payment instrument, and can also be stolen or credentials
given away.
To qualify for a refund for an unauthorized mobile phone payment, the user must:
1. provide us with a credible story on what happened.
2. provide us with a police report.
3. not act fraudulently or are complicit in fraud.
4. not breach the terms & conditions with gross negligence.
The right to a refund is lost if the user intentionally or with gross negligence breached the
terms & conditions of the bunq that were described earlier.
Whether or not intent is at play is usually relatively straightforward. The user has to know
about the violation and should be aware of the fact that his actions will most likely lead to
damages.
The threshold for assessment of gross negligence in these cases must be higher since the
user ought to have been aware of the loss of a mobile phone earlier than a wallet/card.
This in combination with the possibility to initiate transactions only with both the mobile
phone and login codes sets the threshold for disproving gross negligence very high for
the user.
We consider the following cases of unauthorized mobile phone payments as gross
negligence:
● You provided the pin code of Mobile Phone/App login codes to a third party or kept
the codes in an easily accessible place to third parties;
● You checked your bank account within two weeks (for business users: one week)
after the unauthorized transactions but did not notify bunq within that period;
● You notified bunq more than two weeks (for business users: one week) after the
unauthorized transactions and have no credible explanation for why you did not
check your bank account sooner;

7. How to request a refund
To request a refund you can follow these steps within the bunq app:
1. Home.
2. Click on the sub-account from which you've made the payment.
3. Select your payment.
4. Scroll down.
5. Tap on Request a Refund/Chargeback.
