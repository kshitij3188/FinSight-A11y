1. Purpose
bunq’s dividend policy concerns the proportion of bunq’s earnings to be paid out to the
shareholders. Net profit can, alternatively, be reinvested into the growth of the deposit base
and loan books. This policy sets out bunq’s approach to this topic, explaining bunq’s
governance structure, when dividend payments are provided, and in what situations such
payments can be prohibited.
2. Scope of policy
This policy applies to bunq b.v. and bunq holding b.v. as entities supervised by De
Nederlandsche Bank (DNB). The entities are obligated to fulfill capital ratios (with regards to
Common Reporting (COREP) on Own Funds and COREP on Leverage Ratio1). bunq’s
subsidiaries can have their own dividend policy if capital requirements are given by other
regulatory institutions or required by the local law of the subsidiary.
3. Governance
The Supervisory Board and Managing Board define the governance structure of bunq to
achieve business objectives as defined in the base case scenario regarding the SREP2
requirements, as well as the Risk Management Framework. The two boards ensure that there
is sufficient capital available for growth of the balance sheet and for potential investments in
new loans and asset types, and that there is sufficient capital available for operational losses.
The role of the Managing Board
The Managing Board is responsible for determining the amount of earnings eligible for
dividend payout, after allocation of capital for potential investments and growth of the
balance sheet. Dividend payout is determined by the Managing Board on an annual basis by
looking at current earnings and economic forecast for the next 3 years. The Management
Board submits the proposed dividend payout to the Supervisory Board for approval.
Considerations taken by the Managing Board are:
- The key objective of bunq is to maximize bunq’s shareholder value;
- The balance between current dividends and future growth, to maximize the overall
value of bunq;

The role of the Supervisory Board
The Supervisory Board has an important, independent role in the dividend payout and gives
formal approval on the proposed dividend payout by the MB.
4. Dividend policy
If bunq’s expected returns on investments are higher than the cost of capital, it will retain the
earnings necessary to re-invest. As a result, shareholders will be paid only the residual
dividends i.e. earnings left after allocating capital for all the potential investments in new
loans. bunq’s dividend payout will always meet regulatory and internal capital thresholds in
order to protect the interests of other stakeholders like depositholders.
5. Prohibition of dividend payments
Dividend payments of any kind are prohibited if there are reasonable grounds to believe that
the payment would breach the regulatory or internal capital adequacy and liquidity
adequacy thresholds. bunq will carefully and effectively communicate such decisions on
dividend payout to the shareholders and DNB. Breaching any of these aforementioned
thresholds will automatically trigger intervention by the bank regulator.
