How do we invest?
Regularly, users ask us how we invest their money. As bunq’s Chief Risk Officer I am
closely involved with the investments so let me explain.
In Freedom of Choice you indicate how you want us to invest your money. The
majority of users have indicated that they prefer bunq’s investments to be safe and
green. In addition, the Dutch Central Bank, our supervisor, has a range of
requirements that we must meet. And finally, we aspire to make a return on our
investments so we can pay interest to you under the MassInterest scheme. Combined
that makes a complex puzzle to solve.
So how does our investment portfolio look like?
Per 31 March 2020 bunq’s investment portfolio is well-spread over different issuer
types: governments, financial institutions, corporates and covered bonds (bonds that
finance residential mortgages).

Explanation: bunq has only investments with a good credit standing. All investments
have been rated by external agencies. AAA is the very best credit rating, one step lower
is AA, then A, then BBB. BBB is still considered to be of good credit worthiness
(‘investment grade’). Lower credit ratings are BB, B, CCC and D, but we don’t have such
investments.
