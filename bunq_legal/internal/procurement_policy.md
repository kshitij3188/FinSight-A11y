1. Introduction
At bunq, we want every purchase - whether it’s software, services, or office supplies - to
reflect our values. This Procurement Policy sets out how we do that.
It outlines the principles and procedures we follow to make sure our procurement is:
● efficient and well-managed;
● legally sound
● aligned with our ESG commitments, and
● rooted in ethical financial behavior, including responsible tax practices.
This way, we ensure that all our buying decisions support bunq’s mission, protect Eva’s
trust, and contribute to a more sustainable and transparent future.
2. Purpose and Objectives
At bunq, we make sure every purchase is cost-efficient, compliant, and sustainable. This
policy helps us manage risk responsibly, meet regulatory requirements, and stay aligned
with our broader goals – all while making decisions that reflect our values.
3. Scope
This policy applies to bunq b.v., bunq holding b.v., and all subsidiaries and branches. That
includes every entity supervised by De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB).
It covers all stages of procurement, from choosing suppliers to managing contracts and
reviewing performance. Whether we’re buying software, equipment, or services - these
guidelines apply.
4. Procurement Goals
● Ensure cost efficiency by meeting or reducing OPEX forecasts.
● Comply with relevant regulations, including GDPR, DORA, and AML/KYC.
● Promote sustainability in procurement decisions.
● Implement effective risk and supplier management practices.

5. Procurement Principles
5.1 Value for Money
We aim for the best overall outcome - not just the lowest price. That means balancing
cost, quality, risk, and sustainability.
5.2 Fairness and Transparency
All procurement must be open, equal, and free from bias. We treat every potential supplier
with the same respect and opportunity.
5.3 ESG Responsibility
Our buying decisions support Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) goals. We
work with partners who care about sustainability, ethics, and community impact.
5.4 Tax Compliance
We only work with suppliers who practice responsible tax behavior. That means being
transparent, following local tax laws, and steering clear of aggressive tax avoidance
schemes.

8. Procurement Process
We follow a centralized, step-by-step procurement workflow that ensures efficiency,
compliance, and control. The process includes:
Step 1: Purchase Request (PR)
● Initiated by the third-party owner with defined scope, budget, and
justification.
● Requirements include goods/services specification, service levels, and KPIs
(if applicable).
Step 2: Approval Based on Spend Threshold
Role Approval limit
Team Lead Up to €1000
Head Of Up to €10,000
Sector lead Up to €50,000
Management Board Up to €100,000
2 x Management Board Over €100,000
Step 3: Procurement Negotiation
● If the total contract value is above €50,000, the Procurement team must be
involved in supplier selection, negotiation, and documentation review.
● Below this threshold, the third-party owner may manage the process
independently, with optional procurement support.
Step 4: Purchase Order (PO) Issuance
● A Purchase Order is issued only after internal approvals.

9. ESG Integration in Procurement
At bunq, ESG isn’t an afterthought - it’s part of how we choose who to work with. Our
procurement process gives preference to partners who share our values and show real
action in these areas:
9.1 Environmental Standards
● We prefer suppliers with certified environmental systems (like ISO 14001).
● We look for efforts to reduce emissions, minimize waste, and use resources
responsibly.
9.2 Social Standards
● All suppliers must protect labor rights - including fair pay, safe conditions, and a
strict no child or forced labor rule.
● We support suppliers that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI), including
those that are minority- or women-owned.
9.3 Governance Standards
● Suppliers should have strong internal controls, anti-corruption policies, and clear
ownership structures.
● Any potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed and managed.
9.4 ESG Due Diligence
● We conduct formal ESG checks for any supplier with a contract above €50,000.
● For high-risk suppliers, we go further - doing regular follow-ups and performance
reviews to ensure ongoing compliance.
10. Tax Integrity in Procurement
At bunq, we believe in paying our fair share - and we expect the same from the people we
work with. All suppliers must:
● Comply with tax laws in every country where they operate.

● Avoid aggressive tax schemes, like shifting profits to tax havens or using secretive
structures.
● Be ready to share clear tax registration details and ownership information when
needed.
● Cooperate with our obligations under FATCA, CRS, or other international tax
reporting rules.
If a supplier doesn’t meet these expectations - or is involved in unethical tax behavior - we
may remove them from our vendor list.
11. Supplier Code of Conduct
Every supplier working with bunq must follow our Supplier Code of Conduct. It reflects
what we stand for - and what we expect from our partners. This includes:
● Acting with honesty and integrity;
● Respecting human rights and ensuring safe, fair labor conditions;
● Caring for the environment and minimizing harm;
● Committing to transparent and responsible tax behavior.
We may perform audits or checks to make sure these standards are followed - because
living up to values is part of doing business with bunq.
12. Supplier Management
● No pre-approved vendor list – all suppliers must follow the process.
● Evaluation criteria include:
○ Compliance (SOC 2, ISA);
○ Financial stability;
○ Sustainability alignment;
○ Ability to meet KPIs and service levels.
● Risk management includes:

13. Compliance & Legal Requirements
● All procurement must comply with GDPR, DORA, AML/KYC.
● Contract Jurisdiction:
○ Dutch law for all contracts with bunq BV;
○ New York law for bunq LLC.
14. Contract Management Guidelines
● Review & Renewal: Regularly assess contracts for fit and value.
● Exit Strategy: All contracts must include termination and transition clauses.
● Record Keeping: Maintain documentation for audit and compliance.
15. Procurement Tools & Systems
● Procurement tracking is managed via Omnea.
● No current integration with accounting systems.
16. Governance and Review
This policy is owned by our Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and reviewed once a year.
We’ll update it whenever necessary - to reflect new ESG standards, tax rules, or internal
risk insights. Because at bunq, we grow with our responsibilities.
