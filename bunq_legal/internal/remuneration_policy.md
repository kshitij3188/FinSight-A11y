1. Introduction
This document outlines the remuneration policy for bunq. The remuneration policy aims
to align employee remuneration with the long-term interests of the bank and its
stakeholders while ensuring that bunq complies with law.
bunq’s Remuneration Policy aims to attract and motivate a dynamic and highly qualified
workforce. To meet this goal, this policy is followed and updated as needed to match the
rapid growth that bunq is undergoing as well as any regulatory requirements.

3. Purpose and Objectives of this Policy
This remuneration policy is to clarify and specify the remuneration of Adams (including
members of the management board (MB)).
The objectives of the remuneration policy are as follows:
● Attract, retain, and motivate talented Adams to drive bunq's long-term success;
● Contribute to long term value creation and support a focus on the long term
interests of its stakeholders, incl Eva’s, Adam’s and shareholders;
● Align incentives for Adams with the bank's risk appetite, values, and strategic
objectives;
● Prevent or discourage Adams from taking more risks than acceptable to bunq;
● Ensure fairness and transparency in remuneration practices;
● Comply with relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards.
4. Scope and legal framework
This policy applies to the remuneration of Adams. This policy consists of two sections: (i)
general principles of remuneration of Adams (excluding MB) and (ii) a specific
remuneration policy that applies to members of the MB.
This policy takes into account the requirements for remuneration following from:
● Dutch Financial Supervision Act (Wft);
● Capital Requirements Directive (CRD) IV;
● European Banking Authority (EBA) Guidelines;
● Dutch Corporate Governance Code (DCGC);
● Regeling beheerst beloningsbeleid Wft 2021;
● CSRD;
● and ESRS.

6. Principles of this Remuneration Policy
a. All Adams will be equally remunerated for equal work or work of equal value,
irrespective of their gender. bunq further ensures equal opportunities for all
genders by annually assessing and monitoring the gender pay gap in its
organization at a global level.
b. bunq has a Diversity and Equal Treatment Policy - The introduction of the Diversity
and Equal Treatment Policy in July 2023, as mentioned in the social section, also
represents a governance enhancement. This policy formalizes bunq's commitment
to diversity, equality, and inclusion, establishing clear governance structures to
oversee and implement these principles.
c. bunq only awards a severance payment in connection with (excellent) services
rendered and never in relation to failure on the part of the Adam. The foregoing
does not apply in case of a dismissal due to changes in the strategy of bunq, a
merger with or a takeover of bunq or in case the severance payment is determined
by a judge. A severance payment shall be based upon the Dutch transition
allowance (transitievergoeding) since 1 July 2015. A severance payment shall never
exceed an Adam’s year salary, unless specifically required by law.
d. bunq does not award retention, exit and/ or welcome packages.
e. Adams will under no circumstances use personal hedging strategies or enter into
insurance arrangements that may potentially undermine any of the purposes set
out in section 1.1 of this Remuneration Policy.
f. Adams in control functions (the (i) Finance Officer, (ii) Risk Officer, (iii) Compliance
Officer, (iv) Legal Officer, (v) Internal Auditor, (vi) Credit Risk Officer and (vii)
Regulatory Officer) are independent from the business activities of bunq and are
rewarded on the basis of predetermined objectives (i) with respect to their
function and (ii) that are not associated with the results of the activities they
supervise.
g. bunq also offers a pension plan for Adams.

7. Fixed Pay
When determining the remuneration of Adams, bunq can include the following aspects:
● educational, professional and training requirements, skills, effort and responsibility;
work undertaken and the nature of tasks involved;
● the place of employment and its costs of living;
● the hierarchical level of staff and managerial responsibilities of staff;
● the scarcity of staff available in the labor market for specialized positions;
● the length of professional experience of staff;
● professional certifications of staff.
8. Variable Remuneration
a. bunq has no special variable remuneration policy for Identified Staff.
b. A “monthly performance bonus” can be awarded to Adams in the Operational
Team. This bonus is calculated based on Adam's performance in the given month.
c. A profit sharing incentive can be awarded to Adams in non operational teams (with
the exception of the MB) working at bunq or a subsidiary. If a quarter is closed with
10% more profit than bunq’s previous highest profit quarter, the team will be split
into groups based on performance and bonus awarded as per the profit sharing
guidelines.

9. Management Incentive Plan (MIP)
Although MIP is not considered a form of remuneration, the policy mentions this for
transparency reasons.
Consideration is that the MIP is offered to a predetermined group of Adams at fair value.
This access is not dependent on a certain "performance" or achievement. Any deviation
hereof needs to be approved by the RemCo.
10. Specific remuneration policy for MB
The Remuneration of the members of the MB consists of a fixed part. No variable pay is
awarded to the MB. However, they are offered a chance to participate in the MIP.
The total remuneration of the MB is below the median of comparable positions in the
financial sector.
Terms of employment / services and severance pay for MB
