Summary
This policy outlines how bunq classifies and protects my data to ensure confidentiality,
integrity, and availability. It provides clear guidelines on data handling, security measures,
and periodic reviews to keep my information safe.
Section 1 introduces the purpose of this policy and why securing your data matters.
Section 2 defines the scope and responsibilities of this policy.
Section 3 outlines the roles of Data Owners, System Owners, and all Adams in ensuring
data security.
Section 4 explains how your data is classified based on sensitivity and risk, ensuring it’s
protected at the right level.
Section 5 details the Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) framework, which
ensures your data is accessible only to the right people, remains accurate, and is always
available when needed.

implementing control measures accordingly.
Section 7 maps out the data lifecycle stages, from creation to secure destruction,
ensuring your information is handled responsibly at every step.
Section 8 explains the quarterly review procedure, where Data Owners reassess security
classifications to keep protections up to date.
This policy ensures that your data is consistently protected and that every Adam at bunq
knows how to manage it responsibly.

1. Introduction
As a bank that’s also a tech company, bunq processes huge amounts of data every day.
But it’s not just numbers—it’s your financial life. Protecting that is more than a regulation;
it’s a commitment.
To keep your data secure, bunq classifies information based on its sensitivity, following the
Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) model. This ensures every piece of
information—from your account details to your transactions—is handled with the right
safeguards at the right level.
This Data Classification Policy works hand in hand with bunq’s broader Information
Security Policy and aligns with its Risk Appetite, all while meeting the strict requirements
of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
In short: your data is safe, your privacy is respected, and you can bank with peace of mind.
Because that’s exactly what you should expect from the Bank of The Free.

5. Data Classification
Data Classification Scheme is an overview of all data types currently processed within
bunq. Data classification happens in three key phases:
1. Identify what data and information systems are in use.
2. Evaluate the need for protection based on the Confidentiality, Integrity, and
Availability (CIA) model, then classify the data accordingly.
3. Apply the right safeguards, including updating existing security settings if needed.
Identify Data
Every type of data is classified and logged by its Data Owner in the data classification
scheme (and the system itself on the “System and Data Owners” wiki page).
Review settings
Your data security isn’t a one-time setup—it’s a continuous process. That’s why System
Owners and Data Owners regularly check if bunq’s security settings are still a perfect
match for the data they’re protecting.. These reviews cover all security layers (network,
frontend, application and database) and include, but are not limited to:
● Access control;
● User account management (logistical access);
● Firewalls;
● Encryption;
● (Automated) monitoring activities;
● Communication settings (transfer of data); and
● Backups.
Amend system settings
If a new security rating differs from its previous classification, the System Owner and Data
Owner adjust the necessary security settings to ensure continuous protection.

6. Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA)
bunq’s data classification framework is built on three fundamental pillars of information
security:
1. Confidentiality
Your data is only accessible to the right people. Under Article 5(1)(f) of the GDPR, bunq
ensures that your personal information is protected from unauthorized access, keeping it
safe from prying eyes.
2. Integrity
Your data stays accurate, complete, and unaltered. Also covered under Article 5(1)(f) of the
GDPR, this means bunq prevents accidental loss, destruction, or unauthorized changes, so
you can always trust that what you see is correct.
3. Availability
Your data is always there when you need it. Whether you’re making a payment, checking
your balance, or managing your finances, bunq ensures that your data is accessible and
reliable - because banking should just work.
7. Impact Assessment and Control Measures
To properly classify and protect data, each Data Owner assesses how a breach in
Confidentiality, Integrity, or Availability (CIA) would impact bunq’s operations.
● The assessment follows the CIA matrix (shown below), which rates data impact
from 0 (lowest) to 3 (highest).
● This rating determines the necessary security measures, ensuring data is only
accessed by authorized users, remains accurate, and is available when needed.
● If Confidentiality, Integrity, or Availability is compromised - whether accidentally or
intentionally—bunq could suffer financial or reputational damage.

8. Data Lifecycle Stages
bunq carefully tracks how your data flows—from creation to archival or deletion—
ensuring it’s handled safely at every step. By mapping out which teams interact with your
data, bunq identifies risks, strengthens protections, and tailors training so every Adam
knows how to manage confidential data responsibly. This ensures your information stays
secure, only reaching those who need it.
Here’s the data lifecycle at bunq:
● Creation: Document the origin of data, including how and why it is created.
● Storage: Record where and how the data is stored, including security measures in
place.
● Use: Specify the intended use of the data within the organization.
● Sharing: Detail any instances where data is shared outside the organisation,
including with third-party apps or external entities.
● Archiving: State the criteria for moving data to an archive and the security
measures for archived data.
● Destruction: Describe the process for securely destroying data that is no longer
needed.
● Third-Party Sharing Guidelines:
● Approval Process: Establish a clear approval process for sharing data with third
parties.
● Data Flow Chart Update: Require that the data flow chart be updated whenever
data is shared with a new third party. This chart should illustrate the journey of the
data from creation to destruction, including any external touchpoints.
● Data Sharing Agreements: Implement formal data sharing agreements with third
parties that outline how the data should be handled, ensuring alignment with your
organisation’s data protection policies.
● Security and Compliance Checks: Conduct regular checks to ensure third-party
handling of data complies with security standards and regulatory requirements.

● Audit Trails: Maintain audit trails for data shared with third parties, documenting
who accessed the data, for what purpose, and the duration of access.
● Keeping Everyone Informed
● Internal Communication: Ensure relevant internal stakeholders are informed about
the data sharing, including the nature of the data, the recipient, and the purpose
of sharing.
● External Communication: If applicable, inform the data subjects (e.g., customers,
employees) about the sharing of their data, especially if it’s required by privacy laws
like GDPR.
● Training and Awareness: Provide training to employees on proper data-sharing
practices and the importance of updating the data flow chart.
9. Review Procedure
To keep your data secure and properly classified, bunq reviews its systems every quarter.
Data Owners assess the type of data stored or processed and update the system’s security
rating based on the most sensitive data it handles (as defined in Section 5 – CIA).
What happens during the review?
1. Assess security – Data Owners check if the system’s security matches the required
protection level.
2. Document findings – All updates are logged in GitLab issues and compiled into a
report.
3. Approval & closure – Once reviewed by the Head of Controls, the quarter’s review is
officially closed.
This ensures your data is regularly checked, properly classified, and always protected.
