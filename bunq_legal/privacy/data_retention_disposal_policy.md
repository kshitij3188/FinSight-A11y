1. Introduction
At bunq, we know your data is personal, and we take that seriously. We only keep it as
long as needed—to give you the best banking experience, stay compliant with the rules,
and keep improving.
Every piece of data has a clear retention period, meaning we never hold onto your
information longer than necessary. This way, you get the best of both worlds: a bank that’s
smart with your data and respects your privacy.
Once we no longer need your data, we either delete or anonymize it, depending on what
the law requires. This policy outlines exactly how long we keep different types of
information and what happens next—whether it’s securely archived or removed entirely.
Of course, we follow GDPR and all other important regulations, including laws on
anti-money laundering, tax, and company records. And if the rules change, we update our
policies to stay fully compliant—so you never have to worry.
2. Purpose and Objectives of Data Retention
At bunq, privacy and transparency go hand in hand. That’s why we only keep the data
that’s absolutely necessary - to ensure compliance, protect user safety, and give you peace
of mind. Clear retention policies mean you always know when your data will be deleted,
reinforcing trust in how we handle your information.
In some cases, data needs to be retained even after it’s no longer actively used - such as
when a user or employee ends their relationship with bunq. Keeping a complete audit
trail is essential for meeting regulatory requirements set by financial supervisory
authorities like the Dutch Central Bank and the Authority for Financial Markets. This data
is also crucial for internal and external audits, ensuring accountability and transparency.
Beyond compliance, data retention supports essential operational functions. Our systems
provide detailed reporting that helps regulators and auditors in their oversight
responsibilities, while also ensuring that bunq employees operate with professionalism
and integrity. Additionally, securely stored data allows us to respond efficiently to legal
information requests, such as e-discovery in financial investigations or civil legal
proceedings.

Of course, bunq follows all relevant laws and regulations, including those covering tax,
anti-money laundering, and civil law. For example, under the Wwft (Dutch Anti-Money

for a minimum of five years.
3. Retention
bunq believes in keeping data only for as long as necessary - nothing more, nothing less.
To stay compliant with GDPR and the Dutch GDPR Implementation Act, we apply a
10-year retention period for users’ personal data. This approach helps balance regulatory
requirements, operational needs, and risk management. For job applicants, the same
retention period ensures a structured application process, preventing duplicate
submissions and improving recruitment.
Some data, however, must be deleted earlier. The Wwft (Dutch Anti-Money Laundering
and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act) requires certain data to be erased 5 years after the
end of a business relationship. According to Article 34a(3) Wwft, personal data collected
under this law must be deleted immediately after the required period unless another
legal obligation applies.
bunq also retains some data based on legitimate business interests, specifically to
minimize operational risks - such as ensuring compliance and preventing issues caused
by outdated or incomplete records. At present, our technical infrastructure does not fully
support the automatic deletion of all data the moment an account is closed. To manage
this risk, bunq retains most data for 10 years, ensuring full transparency by informing users
of this at the time of collection or whenever requested.
That said, users always have the right to request data deletion. When a request is made,
bunq manually reviews whether the data must still be kept for legal reasons or if it is
necessary for legitimate business purposes. If neither applies, the data is deleted.
To further strengthen privacy protection, bunq is actively working on an automated
deletion system that will permanently remove data as soon as the retention period ends.
Since bunq’s first accounts were created in 2015, the first automatic deletions will take
place in 2025 and onwards.
Security is key, so bunq applies strict technical and organizational safeguards to all
retained data. Once the retention period expires, we take the necessary steps for each

4. Disposal
At bunq, data isn’t kept a moment longer than necessary. Once it reaches the end of its
retention period, we ensure secure and thorough disposal, whether it’s digital or physical.
4.1 Digital Data
When data is no longer needed, our systems take care of it automatically - permanently
removing it from our databases. Wherever possible, any associated logs or backups are
also deleted to ensure complete and proper disposal.
4.2 Physical Data
If physical data ever comes into play - such as returned payment cards - bunq ensures
secure shredding once the data owner approves it. Every shredding process is carefully
documented, keeping a clear record of what was destroyed and why.
5. Final Provisions
5.1 Employee Responsibility
While bunq’s systems handle most data automatically, some things still require a human
touch. Every employee is responsible for managing their own documents in line with this
policy. If they leave bunq, they must ensure that any data or know-how stored outside
bunq’s systems - such as on personal devices - is either securely transferred or deleted.
This applies to physical documents as well. To confirm everything is in order, they
complete the HR leaver form as part of the offboarding process.
5.2 Responsibility & Review
Ensuring this policy stays effective and up to date falls under the Compliance
Department, specifically the Regulatory Compliance/Data Protection Officers. They work
closely with different teams across bunq to review and adjust policies and workflows as
needed, keeping everything compliant and efficient.
