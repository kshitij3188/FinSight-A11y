1. Purpose
At bunq, we believe in doing things the right way - not just because we have to, but
because we want to. That includes how we handle taxes.
This policy explains how we deal with tax matters across our business. It's built around one
key principle:
We pay our fair share. Always.
Whether it’s in the Netherlands, or anywhere else we operate, we follow the rules, avoid
aggressive tax strategies, and contribute to the communities we’re part of.
The goal of this policy is simple: to lay out how bunq approaches tax matters across all
jurisdictions where we operate.
We aim to be:
● Transparent - no hidden tactics or risky loopholes;
● Fair - paying what’s due, where it’s due;
● Efficient - smart structuring that respects the rules;
● Sustainable - always thinking long term.

4. Tax Principles
We’ve boiled down our approach to tax into a few key principles that guide all our
decisions:
Compliance
bunq is committed to complying with all applicable tax laws, regulations, and disclosure
requirements in the jurisdictions where it operates.
Transparency
bunq aims to maintain transparent and cooperative relationships with tax authorities,
providing accurate and timely information.

Risk Management
bunq identifies, assesses, and manages tax risks as part of its overall risk management
framework. Tax positions with material risk are escalated to senior management and/or
the Board.
bunq has a management system which results in immediate actions if suspicions arise
that employees or suppliers are guilty of facilitating tax avoidance or evasion.
Transfer Pricing
bunq adheres to the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines, ensuring that intercompany
transactions are priced on an arm's length basis.
Tax Planning
bunq engages in responsible tax planning aligned with its commercial business activities.
It does not engage in artificial or aggressive tax arrangements intended solely for tax
avoidance.
Tax rulings and government grants
bunq does not have any specific tax rulings with the Dutch tax authorities or in any other
jurisdiction. bunq receives a government grant related to WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur-
en Ontwikkelwerk).
Tax ethics
bunq does not have subsidiaries, branches or associates in jurisdictions with no or zero
corporate tax or in jurisdictions with harmful corporate tax practices, unless they have
substance and their profits are generated from local economic activities.
bunq focuses its international enterprise structure and their international transactions in a
way that reflects the economic substance of the activities and transactions undertaken,
without any steps made primarily to secure a tax advantage.
bunq does not provide financial services to companies in tax havens, unless the company
has substance and its profits are generated from local economic activities.
When bunq has activities in jurisdictions with no or zero corporate tax or in jurisdictions
with harmful corporate tax practices, it will publish in its Tax Report an explanation of the
activities, functions and ultimate shareholder of every subsidiary, branch, joint venture, or

1. Purpose
This section of the tax policy governs how bunq manages its Corporate Income Tax (CIT)
obligations in the Netherlands and other jurisdictions where it operates. It ensures

practices.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all legal entities within bunq b.v. and bunq holding b.v. that are
subject to Dutch CIT or equivalent foreign taxes on corporate profits.

3. Policy Principles
Principle What It Means
Full Compliance We follow the Dutch Corporate Income Tax Act (Wet
op de vennootschapsbelasting 1969) and
international treaties.
Timely & Accurate All CIT returns are submitted correctly and on time,
with strong documentation to back them up,
especially for intercompany financing and service
arrangements.
Fair Transfer Pricing We adhere to the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines.
Intra-group transactions are priced at arm’s length
and documented accordingly, especially for
intercompany financing and service arrangements.
For the subsidiaries and/or branches that are not
revenue generating entities, bunq applies a cost plus
approach to ensure honest taxes are paid in the
different jurisdictions.
Standard Tax Rates We apply the official Dutch rates (19% up to €200k,
25.8% above), subject to changes by the Dutch tax
authority.
Responsible Use of Tax Losses & Incentives We responsibly utilize tax losses carried forward or
carried back and apply tax incentives (e.g., innovation
box) only where justified under the law and aligned
with bunq’s actual economic activities.
No Aggressive Planning We do not engage in artificial arrangements lacking
commercial substance or created solely to avoid
paying CIT.

5b. Value Added Tax
1. Purpose
This section of the tax policy governs how bunq manages its Value Added Tax (VAT)
obligations. It ensures compliance with the Dutch VAT Act (Wet op de omzetbelasting
1968) and EU VAT Directive, while minimizing VAT risks and ensuring proper recovery of
input VAT where allowed.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all Dutch and foreign entities within bunq b.v and bunq holding b.v.
that are subject to VAT obligations or have partial VAT recovery rights. It covers:
● Taxable and exempt services;
● Input VAT recovery;
● VAT invoicing and recordkeeping;
● Reverse charge obligations.
3. Policy Principles
Principle What It Means
Compliance Full compliance with Dutch VAT law and EU VAT
regulations.
Correct VAT Treatment We distinguish between taxable and exempt services
(e.g. loans vs. consultancy).
Input VAT Recovery Implement a pro-rata recovery method (pro rata
BTW-aftrek) for input VAT, consistent with bunq’s mix
of exempt and taxable services. Where applicable,
allocate overheads using a fair and supportable
allocation key.
Registration & Reporting VAT (registration, returns & liabilities) are filed, paid,
and reported without delay in all jurisdictions.
Reverse Charge Properly apply the reverse charge for intra-EU and
international service purchases to avoid
underreporting VAT liabilities.
Documentation VAT invoices and records are kept for at least 7 years.

5d. Other Tax Matters and Tax Reporting
1. Purpose
This section of the tax policy governs how bunq manages its compliance with a range of
other tax obligations and tax-related regulatory reporting, including withholding tax,
financial sector levies, transaction taxes, and international disclosure obligations such as
DAC6, CESOP, CRS, and FATCA.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all entities within bunq b.v. and bunq holding b.v. and covers:
● Dividend, interest, and royalty withholding taxes;
● Dutch bank tax (bankenbelasting);
● Insurance premium tax;
● Real estate transfer tax;
● International tax reporting (DAC6, CRS, FATCA);
● Local and sector-specific levies (e.g., resolution levy).
3. Policy Framework
3.1 Withholding Tax (WHT)
● Obligations: bunq complies with Dutch and treaty-based withholding tax
obligations on dividends, interest, and royalties.
● Relief Mechanisms: Treaty benefits and EU directives are applied only where
sufficient documentation and beneficial ownership evidence is in place.
● Filing and Reporting: Withholding taxes are declared and remitted accurately and
timely to the Belastingdienst.
3.2 Financial Sector Taxes
● Bank Levy: bunq assesses and pays, if applicable, the annual Dutch bank levy
based on its balance sheet total as prescribed by law.

● Resolution Fund Contributions: If required, bunq contributes to the Single

4. International Tax Reporting
4.1 DAC6 (EU Mandatory Disclosure)
● Scope: All cross-border arrangements are reviewed for potential reporting under
the EU Directive on Administrative Cooperation (DAC6).
● Responsibility: The finance and legal teams collaborate to identify and report
qualifying arrangements to the Dutch Tax Authority within 30 days.
4.3 CESOP (Central Electronic System of Payment Information)
● Scope: All cross-border payments made to payees in the EU as defined in
Regulation (EU) 2020/283 and Directive (EU) 2020/284.
● Reporting: Quarterly reports are submitted to the Belastingdienst or international
equivalent where applicable.
4.3 CRS (Common Reporting Standard)
● Due Diligence: Account holders are subject to CRS classification and due diligence
processes.
● Reporting: Annual reports are submitted to the Belastingdienst covering
reportable accounts and jurisdictions.
4.4 FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)
● Classification: bunq complies with U.S. FATCA obligations as a participating
financial institution.
● TIN Collection & Reporting: U.S. persons are identified, and relevant accounts are
reported to the Dutch Tax Authority for onward transmission to the IRS.
4.5. Governance and Controls
● Finance Department: Responsible for identifying, assessing, and reporting on
these other tax obligations.
