## Page 2

At bunq, we always pay taxes at the standard rates in every country where we operate. We
don’t use offshore jurisdictions (often called “tax havens”) to lower our tax bills — because
we believe in doing our fair share, everywhere we do business.
We keep things simple and transparent. bunq operates only within the European
Economic Area and Türkiye, with the following organizational structure:
At bunq, we maintain open and constructive relationships with tax authorities, and we
work closely with them to ensure full compliance with all applicable regulations. We’re
committed to transparency and cooperation at every step.
We can also confirm that, during the reporting period, neither bunq nor any of its
subsidiaries have been involved in any adjudication or arbitration proceedings related to
tax disputes.
Our European branches play an important role by supporting bunq B.V. in providing local
IBANs to our users. These branches do not generate revenue themselves. Instead,
Corporate Income Tax for these branches is calculated based on a cost-plus model. This
approach is fully documented in both our Master and Local Transfer Pricing files, in line
with international standards.
We apply the Authorized OECD Approach (“AOA”) as outlined in the OECD 2010 Report on
the Attribution of Profits to Permanent Establishments.

In the Netherlands, bunq paid actual Corporate Income Taxes of €19,555,902 in 2024 (2023:
€Nil). In 2024, we also used tax deductions from unutilized tax losses, totaling €10,876,753
(2023: €5,128,803). The difference between the tax expenses shown in the earlier table and
the amounts already paid will be settled in 2025.
In Ireland, actual Corporate Income Taxes paid in 2024 were €1,000,938 (2023: €Nil). In
2024, bunq also applied tax deductions from unutilized tax losses of €76,708 (2023:
€427,341).

ensures that fair taxes are paid in each jurisdiction where our activities take place.
