With a bunq Term Deposit, you can set money aside for a fixed period and earn a stable
return, so your savings can grow while you focus on what matters most.
These terms explain how Term Deposits work, your rights and responsibilities, and how we
handle your funds. We’ve written them in plain language to make everything as clear as
possible.
By opening a Term Deposit, you agree to these terms and to bunq’s General Terms and
Conditions. If anything in these terms conflicts with the General Terms, these Term
Deposit Terms apply.
1. What is a Term Deposit
A Term Deposit is a fixed-term savings account that lets you earn interest on your money
over a set period of time. A Term Deposit is not a payment account.
When you open one, you choose how long you want to lock in your savings. For example,
you can lock in your savings for a period of 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, or 24 months.
During that period, you will not be able to add any additional funds to your Term Deposit.
You can open an extra Term Deposit at any time. Your Term Deposit will earn a fixed
interest rate, and you can’t freely withdraw them without paying a penalty.
2. Who can open a Term Deposit
You can open a Term Deposit if you have an active Personal Account (Free, Core, Pro, or
Elite). It’s not possible to open a Term Deposit using Joint or Business Accounts.
There are no fees for opening or maintaining a Term Deposit.
3. Minimum and maximum amounts
The minimum deposit is €1,000 and the maximum per Term Deposit is €100,000. This also
means that the total balance across all your savings (Term Deposits + Savings Accounts)
cannot exceed €100,000 per user. You will not earn interest on any amount above this
limit. This only applies to Savings Accounts in EUR. Any Savings Accounts in other
currencies do not count towards this limit and are subject to their own limits and
interest rates.

4. How interest works
4.1 Interest on your Term Deposit
The interest rate is fixed when you open your Term Deposit, based on the date and term
you choose. You’ll see the rate in the app when opening the deposit.
Interest is calculated as an Annual Percentage Rate (APR) and rounded to the nearest
cent. Interest is paid out at the end of the term, together with your initial deposit.
When the term ends, your total balance (Term Deposit + earned interest) will be paid out
to your oldest Monetary Account, or your Billing Account if you selected one (Billing
Account). The account for the Term Deposit will then be closed.
We may adjust the interest rates applicable to new Term Deposits at any time. Such
changes will not affect your existing Term Deposit, which will continue to earn interest at
the fixed rate agreed at the start.
An example hereof can be found in Annex I.
4.2 Interest allocation
We pay interest on a total balance of up to €100,000 across all your Term Deposits and
Savings Account combined.
Interest on your Term Deposits will always be paid out before any interest on your Savings
Accounts. This means that if the combined total of your Term Deposits and Savings
Accounts exceeds €100,000, you may not receive interest on your full savings balance. If
you only have a Joint Savings Account, we count half of the total balance of that account
towards your personal interest limit.
Further clarification and an example calculation of this can be found in Annex I.
5. Withdrawing your money early
We understand that life happens. If you need to end your Term Deposit before it matures,
you can, but there will be a penalty.
If you close your Term Deposit early, we’ll charge:
1. 1% of the deposit amount for each remaining year of the term, with partial years
counting as full years up to a maximum penalty of 6% of the deposited amount.

If you withdraw early, you’ll receive your full deposit minus the penalty. You won’t earn any
interest on the deposit. You can only withdraw the complete amount. Partial withdrawals

We want you to feel confident. If you change your mind within 14 days of opening your

6. Exceptions: when you can withdraw early without a
penalty
We will waive the early withdrawal penalty when we confirm that special circumstances
apply. If any of the following apply to your situation, please reach out to our Support team
with the necessary documentation.
1. Unemployment: You lose your job and are receiving government unemployment
benefits. Provide a copy of your benefit decision letter and a recent account
statement showing the unemployment payment. This documentation must show
that the unemployment situation occurred after the Term Deposit was opened.
2. Disability: You become (fully or partially) unable for work and are receiving official
disability or incapacity payments. Provide a copy of your benefit decision letter and
a recent account statement showing the disability payment. This documentation
must show that the disability situation occurred after the Term Deposit was
opened.
3. Death: In the event of your death, your heirs must contact bunq to arrange
withdrawal within one (1) year of the date of passing. Provide an official death
certificate and proof of authority (such as a will, grant of probate, or similar
document) within the same time.
4. Court-approved debt restructuring: You are formally admitted to a court-approved
personal debt restructuring program. Provide a copy of the court’s admission or
approval judgment. This documentation must show that the debt restructuring
was entered into after the Term Deposit was opened.
5. Voluntary debt settlement: You are officially participating in a recognized debt
counseling or restructuring process supervised by an accredited debt counselor,
municipality, or similar authority. Provide confirmation from the relevant institution

7. Managing your Term Deposit
You can view and download a statement of your Term Deposit balance and remaining
duration of your Term Deposit anytime in the bunq app.
You can also open multiple Term Deposits with different terms and amounts, as long as
the total balance across all your Term Deposits doesn’t exceed €100,000. This maximum
threshold exists in addition to the €100,000 maximum thresholds in Article 3.
8. When your Term Deposit ends
At the end of the term, we’ll pay out your deposit and interest to your Billing Account
automatically. If your payout cannot be completed (e.g., due to your account being
closed), we’ll safely hold the funds on a temporary account in your name until you contact
us. No interest is earned during that holding period. You can then continue using the
funds as you wish or open a new Term Deposit.
9. Changes to these Terms
We retain the right to update these terms from time to time. If the change benefits you,
we’ll apply it immediately. If the change is to your disadvantage, we will inform you
beforehand through the app and via email, and any such changes will go into effect two
(2) months after we communicate them to you. You will then have the right to close your
Term Deposit early and withdraw your funds without any penalty.
We also reserve the right to end your Term Deposit before the end of the term if you fail to
comply with bunq’s general Terms and Conditions, for instance in case of fraud, AML/KYC
issues, unlawful use, sanctions, and account closure. If that happens, you'll no longer
accrue interest, from the day we inform you that we are ending your Term Deposit. The
penalty from Article 5 will also apply: 1% of your deposit amount for each remaining year of
the term (with partial years counting as full years), up to a maximum of 6% of your total
deposit.

Annex I: Examples and Calculations
4.1 Interest on your Term Deposit
For example: If you open and agree to a 12-month Term Deposit on 1 January 2026 with an
interest rate of 2%, and we change the rate for new 12-month Term Deposits to 1.8% on 4
January 2026, your existing Term Deposit will still earn 2% until it matures.
Should you decide to open another 12-month Term Deposit on 4 January 2026, that new
Term Deposit will earn the new interest rate of 1.8%.
4.2 Interest Allocation
a. Interest over maximum €100,000
For example: You have €80,000 in Term Deposits and €35,000 in your Savings Accounts.
This means you have a total savings balance of €115,000, and will earn interest as follows:
1. You’ll earn interest on the full €80,000 in Term Deposits;
2. After that, you’ll earn interest over the first €20,000 of your Savings Account;
3. You won’t receive interest on the remaining €15,000 of your balance on your
Savings Account, as this exceeds the €100,000 maximum.
b. Interest Allocation with Joint Savings Accounts
If you only have a Joint Savings Account, we count half of the total balance of that account
towards your personal interest limit.
For example: You have €80,000 in Term Deposits and €70,000 in a Joint Savings Account.
We count half of that joint balance, which is €35,000, towards your total amount eligible
for interest. This means you have a total balance of €115,000, and will earn interest as
follows:
1. You’ll earn interest on your €80,000 in Term Deposits;
2. After that, you’ll earn interest on €20,000 of your Savings Account;
3. No interest will be paid on the remaining €15,000 of your share in the Joint Savings
Account, as this exceeds the €100,000 maximum.
This doesn’t affect the other account holder’s share. If their total balance (including their
half of the joint account) is below €100,000, they’ll still earn interest on their full eligible
amount.
